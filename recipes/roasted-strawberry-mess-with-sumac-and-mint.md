# Roasted Strawberry Mess with Sumac and Mint

**Source:** [Yotam Ottolenghi's Crowd Pleaser, Menu 1: The Full Roast Dinner](https://www.channel4.com/programmes/ottolenghis-crowd-pleasers)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 50 minutes
**Yield:** 6 servings

An Eton Mess with a Middle Eastern spin — roasted sumac-and-vanilla strawberries layered with whipped yoghurt cream and crushed shop-bought meringue. Bright, creamy, and impressive with minimal effort.

---

## Ingredients

### Roasted Strawberries

- 600g fresh or frozen strawberries, hulled and halved
- 1½ tbsp sumac, plus extra to finish
- 10g mint sprigs
- 1 vanilla pod, split in half, seeds scraped
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 70g icing sugar
- 80ml water
- A pinch of salt

### Yoghurt Cream

- 440g Greek yoghurt
- 340g double cream
- 60g icing sugar

### To Serve

- Shop-bought meringue, crushed
- Mint leaves, torn

---

## Instructions

1. **Preheat the oven** to 200°C.

2. **Roast the strawberries:** Place all the strawberry ingredients in a large oven tray. Mix well and roast for 20 minutes, stirring gently halfway through, until soft. Remove from the oven and leave to cool completely.

3. **Make the yoghurt cream:** Whisk the yoghurt, double cream, and icing sugar in a large bowl until thickened. Refrigerate until ready to assemble.

4. **Assemble:** Spoon half the cream onto a serving platter. Spoon over some strawberries with their juice, crush over some meringue, and scatter over some torn mint leaves. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used up.

5. **Finish:** Sprinkle over the remaining sumac and serve.
