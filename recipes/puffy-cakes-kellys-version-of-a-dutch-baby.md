# Puffy Cakes (Kelly's Version of a Dutch Baby)

**Cook Time:** 15–20 minutes

A dramatic oven pancake that puffs up with crispy golden edges and a custardy centre. Butter melted directly in the hot pan creates the signature rise without any mixing.

---

## Ingredients

- ½ cup white flour
- ½ cup milk (or milk substitute)
- 2 eggs
- ½ tablespoon vanilla (optional)
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- ¼ cup butter

---

## Method

1. **Preheat and prep pan:** Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Place metal, glass or ceramic 8-in pie pan into the oven while it heats up (with the butter —cut up—in the pan).

2. **Make the batter:** Whisk the egg, milk and sugar together until smooth. Stir in vanilla, if using. Let sit for at least 10–15 minutes.

3. **Pour the batter:** Once the oven is up to temperature and the butter is melted / starting to bubble (check regularly to ensure it's not burning), pour the batter into the pan. DO NOT MIX the batter and the butter.

4. **Bake:** Place back in the oven and bake 15–20 minutes, or until the centre is baked through and the edges are puffed and golden.

5. **Serve with toppings:** Take out of the oven and add desired toppings: Cinnamon and sugar, maple syrup and sweetened yogurt, berries and whipped cream or, my favourite, fresh lemon juice and brown sugar.
