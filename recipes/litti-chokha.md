# Litti Chokha

**Source:** [Dassana's Veg Recipes](https://www.vegrecipesofindia.com/litti-chokha-recipe/)

**Prep Time:** 1 hour
**Cook Time:** 45 minutes
**Yield:** 4 servings

Litti Chokha is a traditional, rustic and popular dish of stuffed whole wheat dough balls filled with a spiced mixture of sattu (roasted gram or roasted black chickpea flour) and later baked or roasted. It is typically served with chokha, such as baingan chokha (eggplant mash) or tomato chokha or potato chokha.

---

## Ingredients

### For litti dough

- 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta) or 240 grams whole wheat flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
- ¾ to 1 cup water or add as required

### For stuffing

- 1 cup roasted black chickpea flour (sattu)
- ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
- ½ teaspoon fennel seeds (saunf, aniseeds)
- ½ teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain, bishops weed)
- ½ teaspoon nigella seeds (kalonji)
- ½ teaspoon red chilli powder
- 1 to 2 teaspoons green chilies – chopped
- 1 teaspoon ginger – finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon garlic – finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons coriander leaves – chopped
- ¼ teaspoon black salt
- salt as per taste
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- 2 teaspoons mustard oil
- 1 to 2 tablespoons of water or add as required

### For Baingan Chokha

- 420 grams eggplant – large or 1 large bharta baingan
- ⅓ cup onions – finely chopped or 1 medium sized or 50 grams
- ½ teaspoon green chilies – chopped
- ½ teaspoon garlic – chopped
- 1 to 2 tablespoons coriander leaves – chopped (cilantro)
- 1 teaspoon mustard oil
- ¼ to ½ teaspoon lemon juice – optional
- salt as required

### For Tomato Chokha

- 200 grams tomatoes or 2 medium to large tomatoes
- ½ teaspoon green chilies – chopped
- ½ teaspoon garlic – finely chopped
- ½ teaspoon lemon juice
- ½ teaspoon mustard oil
- 1 tablespoon coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped

### For serving

- Baingan Chokha
- Tomato Chokha
- Aloo Chokha
- ¼ cup melted ghee or use as required

---

## Instructions

### Preparing litti dough

1. **Mix dry ingredients:** In a mixing bowl, take the whole wheat flour (atta) and salt. Also add ghee or oil.

2. **Knead dough:** Add water in parts and knead to a smooth soft dough. Add ¾ to 1 cup water depending on the quality of flour.

3. **Rest:** Knead till smooth and soft. Cover the dough and keep aside to rest for 20 minutes.

### Making stuffing for litti

4. **Crush spices:** In a mortar-pestle take the cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Lightly crush them and keep aside.

5. **Combine stuffing:** Take the sattu or roasted black chickpea flour in a mixing bowl or pan. Add the slightly crushed cumin seeds and fennel seeds.

6. **Add remaining spices:** Add carom seeds, nigella seeds, red chilli powder, chopped green chilies, finely chopped ginger and garlic, chopped coriander leaves and black salt.

7. **Season and moisten:** Add regular salt as per taste. Add lemon juice and mustard oil. Mix very well.

8. **Adjust consistency:** Sprinkle 1 to 2 tablespoons water all over and mix the flour with water evenly. Add more water if required. The sattu stuffing mixture should not be too dry or too wet. If the mixture is dry, then the dryness is felt when eating litti.

### Stuffing & shaping litti

9. **Divide dough:** After 30 minutes, make small or medium sized balls from the dough.

10. **Roll and fill:** Sprinkling some flour, roll each dough ball to a circle of about 5 to 6 inches. Place 2 to 3 tablespoons of the stuffing in the center.

11. **Shape (method 1):** Pleat and then join the edges. Press the joined part and lightly roll the stuffed dough balls in your palms, so as to get a round shape. Keep aside.

12. **Shape (method 2, alternative):** Flatten the dough ball with your palms and make a shallow cup. Place the stuffing. Gently bring together the outer dough cover in the center while pressing the stuffing. Join and then seal the edges. This is a bit tricky method, so use the method which suits you.

13. **Rest shaped littis:** Keep the prepared littis under a cotton towel or napkin, so that the dough does not dry out.

14. **Arrange for baking:** Prepare all stuffed littis and place them on a baking tray which has been greased with some oil. Grease the baking tray well, otherwise the littis can stick to the tray.

### Baking litti

15. **Preheat and bake:** Place the littis in a preheated oven at 200°C / 390°F. Preheat the oven for 20 minutes at 200°C / 390°F prior to baking.

16. **Flip partway:** After 10 to 15 minutes of baking, remove the tray and flip each litti. Keep the tray back in the oven.

17. **Continue baking:** After another 10 to 15 minutes, remove the baking tray and flip each litti again. Place the tray back in the oven. This has to be done once or twice more for even baking of littis.

18. **Check doneness:** Bake for about 30 to 35 minutes or till the crust looks done with some light brown or golden brown spots on them. Timing will vary upon the size and litres of the oven.

19. **Brush with ghee:** Brush the littis with some melted ghee all over.

20. **Optional char:** Secure the baked litti in a pair of tongs and place it on fire. Rotate for a few seconds till you see some charred spots on litti. This step is optional and only to get charred spots on the litti.

21. **Serve:** Serve Bihari style litti with baingan chokha, aloo chokha and tomato chokha accompanied with a small bowl of melted ghee. The litti is broken, dipped in ghee and then eaten with the chokha.

### Making baingan chokha

22. **Roast eggplant:** Rinse the eggplant in water a few times. Place it on a wired rack on a stovetop or directly on the stovetop flame. Keep turning the eggplant after 2 to 3 minutes so that it is evenly cooked. Roast the eggplant till it is completely cooked and tender.

23. **Check doneness:** With a knife check the doneness — the knife should slide easily in the eggplant. Remove the eggplant and immerse in a bowl of water till it cools.

24. **Peel and chop:** Peel and chop the eggplant. Add the chopped baingan in a mixing bowl along with its juices.

25. **Mix chokha:** Add the finely chopped onions, chopped green chilies, chopped garlic and chopped coriander leaves. Add mustard oil. If you want, add ¼ to ½ teaspoon lemon juice for a slight tang. Season with salt. Mix everything very well.

### Making tomato chokha

26. **Roast tomatoes:** Rinse tomatoes well in water. Place them on a wired grill rack on the stovetop and begin to roast. You can also place them directly on the stovetop flame, or grill or roast in the oven (though you won't get the smoky flavor). Turn them after a few minutes for even roasting — the tomatoes should get charred.

27. **Check doneness:** Roast the tomatoes till they are completely cooked from within. With a knife check the doneness — the knife should slide easily in the tomatoes.

28. **Peel and mix:** Once the tomatoes cool, peel them and remove the charred skin. Chop them and add the chopped tomatoes along with their juices in a mixing bowl. Add chopped green chilies, finely chopped garlic, chopped coriander leaves, mustard oil and lemon juice. Season with salt as per taste. Mix very well. Check the taste and adjust lemon juice, mustard oil, green chilies or salt if required.

### Making aloo chokha

29. **Boil potatoes:** Boil or steam 2 medium potatoes (160 grams) adding water as needed till they are thoroughly cooked. You can steam them in a pan or electric rice cooker, or pressure cook in 1.25 to 1.5 cups water for 5 to 6 whistles in a 2 litre pressure cooker.

30. **Cool and peel:** Allow them to become warm, then peel and mash them.

31. **Mix and serve:** Add ⅓ cup finely chopped onions, ½ teaspoon chopped green chilies, ½ teaspoon finely chopped garlic, ½ teaspoon mustard oil and ½ teaspoon lemon juice. Mix very well. Check the taste and add more salt, lemon juice or green chilies if required. Serve with litti.

## Notes

- Mustard oil is a must in these recipes. Do not skip it.
- If sattu is not available, use roasted chana without the husk (split or whole). Grind ¾ cup of the roasted chana in a grinder jar till fine powder. Sieve it and then use. The whole or split hulled roasted chana is the same chana used in coconut chutneys.
- The approximate nutrition info is for one serving of 4 litti served with Baingan Chokha, Tomato Chokha and ghee.
