# Vinegar-Roasted Potatoes with Herbs

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 65 minutes
**Yield:** 6–8 servings

Potatoes simmered in vinegar before roasting are seasoned all the way through and hold their shape beautifully. Dressed hot with fresh herbs, chilli, and a sharp Maggi-spiked vinegar sauce, they're served straight from the tray with extra dipping sauce on the side.

---

## Ingredients

- 2kg Maris Piper potatoes (or another floury potato), cut into 3–4 cm chunks
- 4 bay leaves
- 10 garlic cloves, skin on
- 60ml white wine vinegar
- 200ml olive oil
- 10g parsley, finely chopped
- 15g dill, finely chopped
- 20g chives, finely chopped
- 1 red chilli, finely sliced
- Fine and flaked sea salt

### Vinegar Dipping Sauce

- 90ml white wine vinegar
- 2 tsp Maggi liquid seasoning, or Worcestershire sauce

---

## Method

1. **Simmer the potatoes:** Place the potatoes, bay leaves, garlic and vinegar in a large pot with 4 tablespoons of fine sea salt. Cover with plenty of water and place on a high heat. Bring to the boil, reduce the heat to medium high and simmer for 20 minutes, until a knife goes through easily. Drain, shaking the colander gently to create craggy edges, and leave to steam dry for 10 minutes, then place in a large roasting tray. Set aside the garlic cloves and discard the bay leaves.

2. **Preheat the oven:** Preheat the oven to 230°C.

3. **Roast the potatoes:** Pour the olive oil over the potatoes, mix gently and roast for 45 minutes, stirring halfway through, and adding the garlic cloves in the last 10 minutes.

4. **Dress and serve:** Mix the ingredients for the vinegar dipping sauce together in a bowl and drizzle half over the potatoes. Mix well, stir through the herbs and chilli and serve on the tray, or arrange on a platter, with the remaining vinegar dipping sauce on the side and sprinkled with some flaked sea salt.
