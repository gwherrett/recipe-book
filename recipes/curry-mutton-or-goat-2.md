# Curry Mutton or Goat #2

**Source:** Recipes from Jamaica — Jill Roberts

**Yield:** 6 servings

A Jamaican curried goat with a rich marinade of aromatics and curry powder, slow-simmered until tender and finished with coconut milk.

---

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds goat meat
- ½ cup onions, sliced
- 1 stalk scallion
- 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 1-inch piece ginger, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 tablespoon black pepper
- 2½ tablespoons curry powder
- 1 hot pepper, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 teaspoon lime juice
- 6 cups boiling water
- ½ cup coconut milk
- 2 ounces butter
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 sprig thyme

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate the meat:** Wash goat meat and cut in pieces, sprinkle with salt and pepper. Add curry, scallion, onion, garlic, ginger and hot pepper. Work these into the meat. Cover, let stand for 1½ hours.

2. **Brown and simmer:** Heat the oil. Add meat and seasonings, sprinkle lime juice over. Fry lightly for five minutes, stirring. Add 4½ cups boiling water, and simmer, covered, 2–3 hours until meat is tender, stirring from time to time. Add water as needed.

3. **Finish:** Add coconut milk, butter, sugar and thyme. Cook for five minutes more.

4. **Serve:** Serve accompanied by plain white rice, roti, chutney, sliced beets, grated coconut, raisins, ripe banana and sliced sweet peppers.
