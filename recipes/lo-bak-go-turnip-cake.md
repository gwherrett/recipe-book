# Lo Bak Go (Turnip Cake)

**Yield:** 1 large cake (serves 6–8)

A classic Cantonese dim sum staple made from shredded lo bak (white radish/daikon) and rice flour, steamed with savoury fillings until firm and sliceable.

---

## Ingredients

- 4–5 lb lo bak (white radish / daikon), shredded
- 400g rice flour
- 4 tablespoons cornstarch
- 4½ cups water (2½ cups for cooking the lo bak, 2 cups for the flour mixture)

### Filling

- Dried shrimp, soaked and finely chopped
- Chinese sausage (lap cheong), diced
- Dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and finely chopped
- Light soy sauce
- Cooking wine
- White pepper
- Chicken stock powder

### Topping

- Green onion, chopped
- Sesame seeds

---

## Instructions

1. **Cook the lo bak:** Place shredded radish in a pot with 2½ cups water and cook over medium heat until softened and translucent. Turn off the heat.

2. **Mix the flour:** In a large bowl, combine rice flour and cornstarch. Gradually add 2 cups water and stir until smooth.

3. **Stir-fry the filling:** Sauté the dried shrimp, diced Chinese sausage, and chopped mushrooms until aromatic. Season with light soy sauce, cooking wine, white pepper, and chicken stock powder.

4. **Combine:** Add the stir-fried filling to the cooked lo bak and stir to incorporate. Gradually pour the flour mixture into the hot radish mixture, stirring thoroughly. The texture should be thick but pourable.

5. **Steam:** Pour the mixture into a greased or parchment-lined steaming tray and smooth the surface. Steam over high heat for 30–40 minutes. Check doneness with a toothpick — it should come out clean.

6. **Cool and serve:** Allow the cake to cool completely before slicing. Garnish with chopped green onion and sesame seeds. Optional: pan-fry slices until golden brown for a crispy texture.
