# Chicken Salan (Pakistani Chicken Shorba Curry)

**Source:** [Fatima Cooks](https://fatimacooks.net/chicken-salan-recipe-pakistani-chicken-curry/)

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes
**Cook Time:** 50 minutes
**Yield:** 4 servings

A classic, comforting chicken curry that no Pakistani kitchen is complete without. Versatile and forgiving — use minimal ingredients and whatever spices you have access to for this hug in a bowl.

---

## Ingredients

- ½ cup (125 ml) oil
- 1 medium (110 g) onion, finely diced
- 2 (240 g) tomatoes, halved
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic
- 1 teaspoon minced ginger
- 2–2½ teaspoon salt, or to taste
- 2 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chilli powder (essential for the colour)
- 1 teaspoon cumin powder
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 2 teaspoon coriander powder
- ½ teaspoon turmeric
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 whole cloves
- 10 whole black peppercorns
- 1 green cardamom
- 1 teaspoon chilli powder (taste before adding and adjust to spice preference)
- 1 medium chicken, bone-in, curry-cut into approx 12–14 pieces (approx 800 g / 1.7 lb)
- ½ teaspoon garam masala
- Chopped coriander or dried fenugreek, to garnish

---

## Instructions

1. **Heat the oil:** Heat the oil in a deep pot.

2. **Cook the onions:** Add the diced onions. Cook on medium-high heat until translucent and lightly browned at the edges.

3. **Steam the tomatoes:** Add the halved tomatoes and a splash of water. Cover and steam on low for about 5 minutes. Once done, the skin should slip off the tomatoes easily — remove and discard.

4. **Build the masala:** Turn the heat up, add the garlic, ginger, and all the spices. Dry out the masala, stirring constantly, mashing the onions and tomatoes with your spoon. Alternatively, blend for a super smooth masala.

5. **Add the chicken:** Once the masala is very dry, the oil begins to separate at the edges, and the masala starts to catch at the bottom, add the chicken.

6. **Sauté the chicken:** Sauté on high heat, stirring constantly, until the chicken changes colour and is no longer pink.

7. **Simmer:** Add 2½ cups of water. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on low for 30 minutes.

8. **Finish:** Once the chicken is cooked through, stir in the garam masala and coriander or dried fenugreek.

---

## Notes

- The whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, peppercorns, cardamom) are optional, although highly recommended.
- Paprika or Kashmiri red chilli powder is used for its mild flavour, allowing a larger quantity for deep colour. If unavailable, use red chilli powder to taste.
