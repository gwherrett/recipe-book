# Chicken with Dates, Saffron and Freekeh

**Source:** [The Guardian / Yotam Ottolenghi](https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/oct/15/one-pot-recipes-meat-grain-lamb-rice-chicken-freekeh-beef-short-rib-barley-yotam-ottolenghi)

**Prep Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes (marinating)
**Cook Time:** 55 minutes
**Yield:** 4 servings

Freekeh is wheat harvested while still green and not completely dry, then roasted — it infuses any dish with a hint of smokiness. This one-pot dish pairs it with saffron-scented chicken thighs and sweet medjool dates.

---

## Ingredients

- 8 chicken thighs, skin on and on the bone
- 2 tsp paprika
- ¾ tsp ground cinnamon
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 medium onions, peeled and cut into 2cm-wide wedges
- 3 small cinnamon sticks
- 3 wide strips orange peel
- 3 bay leaves
- 10 cardamom pods, lightly bruised
- 250g freekeh, rinsed
- 6 medjool dates, quartered lengthways and stoned
- ¼ tsp saffron threads
- 500ml chicken stock
- 25g flaked almonds, toasted
- 5g mint leaves, roughly shredded
- 180g Greek yoghurt, to serve (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate the chicken:** Put the chicken in a large bowl, sprinkle over the paprika, ground cinnamon, three-quarters of a teaspoon of salt and plenty of pepper. Rub the seasoning all over the chicken, then put in the fridge to marinate for an hour and a half.

2. **Fry the chicken:** Heat the oil in a large sauté pan for which you have a lid on a medium-high flame, then fry the chicken for 10 minutes, turning them halfway through, until the skin is dark golden-brown. Transfer the chicken to a plate and discard all but two tablespoons of the oil in the pan.

3. **Char the onions:** Turn up the heat to high, then fry the onions for six to seven minutes, stirring frequently, until slightly charred.

4. **Add the spices:** Turn down the heat to medium, add the cinnamon sticks, orange peel, bay and cardamom, and fry for three minutes, until the onions are soft and the spices are aromatic.

5. **Add freekeh and chicken:** Stir in the freekeh, dates, saffron, stock, 100ml water and three-quarters of a teaspoon of salt, then return the chicken to the pan so it sinks into the freekeh a little but is not entirely submerged.

6. **Simmer:** Once the pot comes to a boil, turn down the heat to medium-low, cover and simmer for 35 minutes, until the freekeh and chicken are cooked.

7. **Finish and serve:** Raise the heat, remove the lid and leave to bubble away for two to three minutes more, until the grain has absorbed most of the liquid. Take the pot off the heat, sprinkle with the almonds and mint, and serve at once with Greek yoghurt alongside, if desired.
