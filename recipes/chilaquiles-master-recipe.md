# Chilaquiles Master Recipe

**Source:** [Rick Bayless](https://www.rickbayless.com/recipe/chilaquiles-master-recipe/)

**Yield:** 4 servings

Chilaquiles are Rick Bayless's go-to comfort food — crispy fried tortilla chips simmered in a brothy salsa until they reach that perfect halfway point between crisp and tender, then topped with crema, cheese, and raw onion. They can be made with practically any sauce, though tomatillo (*verdes*) and tomato (*rojos*) are the most common.

---

## Ingredients

### Sauce

- 2 pounds ripe tomatoes (plum tomatoes make the best-textured sauce), **OR** 2 pounds tomatillos, husked and rinsed
- Fresh hot green chiles to taste (roughly 3 serranos or 2 jalapeños), stemmed
- 1 medium (6-ounce) white onion, cut into ½-inch slices
- 1½ tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2½ cups chicken broth, plus a little extra if needed
- A couple of big sprigs of fresh epazote, plus more for garnish — **OR** a big handful of cilantro leaves, plus more for garnish
- Salt (about 1½ teaspoons)

### Chips

- 12 (about 10 ounces) cold or stale 6-inch corn tortillas, cut into wedges or ½-inch strips, with oil to a depth of 1 inch for frying — **OR** 8 ounces (about 8 cups loosely packed) store-bought tortilla chips

### Optional Protein

- 1½ to 2 cups (roughly 8 to 10 ounces) coarsely shredded cooked chicken, pork, or beef — **OR** 4 sunny-side-up fried eggs

### Garnishes

- About ⅓ cup Mexican crema, crème fraîche, or store-bought sour cream thinned with a little milk
- ¼ cup (or more) finely crumbled Mexican queso añejo or other dry grating cheese such as Romano or Parmesan
- Thinly sliced raw white onion

---

## Instructions

1. **Roast the sauce base:** Spread the tomatoes or tomatillos, chiles, and onion onto a rimmed baking sheet and roast under a preheated broiler until blackening and soft on one side, about 6 minutes. Flip everything over and roast the other side. (Serrano chiles may be blackened and soft before the tomatoes are ready — remove them early if so.) Cool until handleable. If using tomatoes, slip off their blackened skins. Scrape everything, including any accumulated juices, into a blender or food processor and process until nearly smooth.

2. **Cook the sauce:** Heat the oil in a medium (4- to 5-quart) pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. When hot enough to make a drop of the puréed sauce sizzle sharply, add it all at once. Cook, stirring almost constantly, until reduced to the consistency of tomato paste, about 4 to 8 minutes. Stir in the chicken broth and simmer for a few minutes to bring the flavors together. Taste and season with salt, usually about 1½ teaspoons. Stir in the epazote or cilantro. You should have about 4½ cups of brothy sauce; if you have more, simmer until reduced to that quantity, and if less, add water or more broth.

3. **Fry the tortillas (if not using store-bought chips):** If the tortillas seem moist, lay them out in a single layer to dry for a few minutes — otherwise they risk absorbing too much oil. In a deep heavy skillet, heat oil to a depth of 1 inch over medium-high until a piece of tortilla sizzles sharply (about 360°F). Fry the tortilla wedges or strips in batches until crispy and golden. Drain on paper towels.

4. **Add the protein (optional):** If using shredded meat, stir it into the hot sauce now. If serving with fried eggs, cook them in a separate skillet with a little oil until set, sunny-side up, and set aside.

5. **Simmer the chips:** Raise the heat under the sauce to medium-high. Stir in the chips, coating all of them well. Let return to a rolling boil, cover, and turn off the heat. Let stand 1½ to 4 minutes — no longer — until the chips have softened to your liking. They should be a little chewy, definitely not mushy.

6. **Serve:** Gently stir everything together and scoop into deep plates or bowls. Drizzle with the crema, scatter on the raw onion slices, and sprinkle generously with the cheese and extra epazote or cilantro. Top each serving with a fried egg if using.

---

## Notes

- **Tortilla tip:** This is the place for leftover or stale tortillas — it doesn't matter if, when fried, they're a little tough or even slightly greasy. Store-bought chips work equally well.
- **Make-ahead:** The brothy sauce can be completed up to 3 to 4 days ahead and stored covered in the refrigerator. Homemade chips can be made a day or two in advance.
- **Speedy version:** Heat a 16-ounce jar of roasted tomatillo salsa with 2 to 2½ cups of chicken broth. Stir in 8 ounces of chips, let stand a few minutes, then garnish and serve.
- **Variations:** Blanched greens or roasted vegetables can be added with the chips. Cooked chorizo can replace other meats (sprinkle over before the cheese). Diced avocado makes a great garnish, and chopped green onions or grilled onion can replace the raw white onion on top.
