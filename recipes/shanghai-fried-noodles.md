# Shanghai Fried Noodles (Cu Chao Mian)

**Source:** [The Woks of Life](https://thewoksoflife.com/shanghai-fried-noodles/)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**Yield:** 2–4 servings

Shanghai Fried Noodles (上海粗炒面, Shànghǎi cū chǎomiàn) are thick wheat noodles stir-fried with pork, shiitake mushrooms, and leafy greens in a simple soy-based sauce — a quick, satisfying one-pan meal.

---

## Ingredients

### Pork & Marinade

- 6 oz lean pork, sliced into ¼-inch strips
- ¾ tsp cornstarch
- ½ tsp light soy sauce
- ⅛ tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp Shaoxing wine
- ⅛ tsp sugar

### Stir-Fry

- 3 tbsp oil, divided
- 8 shiitake mushrooms, fresh or dried (soak first if dried), thinly sliced
- 1 lb fresh or frozen Japanese-style udon noodles (or Shanghai noodles)
- 2½ tsp dark soy sauce
- 2½ tsp light soy sauce
- ¼ tsp sugar
- 1 bunch choy sum or baby bok choy, washed and trimmed

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate the pork:** Combine the pork strips with the cornstarch, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and sugar. Mix well and set aside while you prepare the remaining ingredients.

2. **Prep all ingredients:** Have everything measured and ready before you turn on the stove — this dish comes together very quickly.

3. **Cook the pork:** Heat a wok over high heat until just starting to smoke. Add 1 tablespoon of oil around the perimeter of the wok. Add the pork and stir-fry until browned. Reduce the heat to medium, remove the pork from the wok, and set aside.

4. **Cook the mushrooms:** Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil to the wok and stir-fry the mushrooms for 2 minutes.

5. **Add noodles and sauce:** Add the noodles and gently break them up with chopsticks. Once the noodles are free, add the dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and sugar. Return the pork to the wok and stir-fry everything together until the noodles are an even, deep brown colour.

6. **Add the greens:** Add the choy sum or bok choy and toss with the noodles until the greens are just wilted. Serve hot.

---

## Notes

- **Noodles:** You need thick noodles for this recipe — fresh or frozen udon noodles work well, as do noodles labelled "Shanghai noodle." If using dried noodles, boil until al dente per package directions, rinse with cold water to remove surface starch, then toss with a little vegetable oil to prevent sticking.
- **Black vinegar:** Some people like to add a few drops of Chinese black vinegar right before eating.
