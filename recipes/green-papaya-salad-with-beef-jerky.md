# Green Papaya Salad with Beef Jerky

**Source:** Hastings Sunrise Cooks (Mr. Red Cafe, by Hong Duong)

**Yield:** 4 servings

A very nice and light snack, or the serving size can be doubled to make it an entree.

---

## Ingredients

### For the papaya salad

- ½ small green papaya, shredded
- 1 large carrot, shredded
- 85 g beef jerky, cut in small pieces (1–2 inch long strips, as thin as possible)
- ¼ cup mint
- ¼ cup cilantro
- ¼ cup lemon balm
- 2 tbsps peanuts

### For the sweet and sour fish-sauce dressing

- 2 tsps fish sauce
- 2 tsps sugar
- 5 tsps water
- 1 tsp vinegar
- 1 tsp mirin cooking sauce
- ½ tsp garlic chili sauce (optional)
- Sriracha hot sauce (optional, amount as desired)

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the dressing:** Mix together all the dressing ingredients and stir well. Let the mixture stand for about 10–15 minutes to become concentrated.

2. **Prepare the salad base:** Mix the green papaya and carrot in a bowl, then set aside.

3. **Prepare the herbs:** Chop and mix the mint, cilantro, and lemon balm in a separate bowl.

4. **Assemble:** For presentation, use a large serving dish and layer the herb mix on the bottom. Add papaya and carrot evenly on the next thick layer. Add the beef jerky on top (or substitutes as noted). Pour the sweet and sour fish-sauce dressing on the top. Garnish generously with peanuts (and/or fried shallots).

---

## Notes

- **Substitutions:** Use fried shallots instead of peanuts. For a vegetarian dish, take out the beef jerky and use deep fried tofu, and replace the fish sauce with soy sauce.
