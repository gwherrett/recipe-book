# Lamb Shawarma (Rotisserie Method, 2 kg)

**Category:** Mains / Middle Eastern & Grilled  
**Yield:** Serves 6–8  
**Cooking Method:** Rotisserie or BBQ (indirect heat)

---

## Ingredients
For **2 kg (4.4 lb)** lamb — use boneless leg, shoulder, or trimmed steaks.

### Marinade
- 90 ml olive oil (≈ 6 tbsp)  
- 90 ml plain yogurt *(optional, for tenderness)*  
- 45 ml red wine vinegar or pomegranate molasses (≈ 3 tbsp)  
- 60 ml lemon juice (≈ juice of 2 lemons)  
- 6 garlic cloves, finely minced  
- 20 ml ground cumin (≈ 4 tsp)  
- 20 ml ground coriander (≈ 4 tsp)  
- 20 ml paprika (≈ 4 tsp)  
- 10 ml ground allspice (≈ 2 tsp)  
- 10 ml ground cinnamon (≈ 2 tsp)  
- 5 ml ground cloves (≈ 1 tsp)  
- 5 ml ground nutmeg (≈ 1 tsp)  
- 5 ml black pepper (≈ 1 tsp)  
- 10 ml salt (≈ 2 tsp)  
- *Optional:* 2 ml Aleppo pepper or chili flakes (≈ ½ tsp)

---

## Method

1. **Marinate**  
   Combine all marinade ingredients in a large bowl. Add lamb slices or cubes, coat thoroughly, cover, and refrigerate 12–24 hours. Bring to room temperature 1 hour before cooking.

2. **Preheat Rotisserie**  
   Set grill / BBQ to **180–200 °C / 350–400 °F** using indirect heat.

3. **Cook**  
   Mount lamb securely on the spit. Roast for **1 h 45 min – 2 h 15 min**, rotating continuously and **basting every 20 min** with leftover marinade or olive oil.  
   Internal temperature:  
   - **60–65 °C / 140–150 °F** = medium  
   - **70 °C / 160 °F** = medium-well

4. **Rest & Slice**  
   Remove from heat, cover loosely with foil, and rest 15–20 minutes. Slice thinly against the grain.

5. **Optional Finish**  
   For a “street-shawarma” effect, briefly sear sliced meat on a hot skillet (1–2 min) to crisp the edges.

## Serving Suggestions

| Category | Accompaniments | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| **Bread** | Pita / Saj / Laffa | Warmed, used to wrap meat |
| **Sauces** | Toum (garlic), Tahini, Yogurt-mint | Adds sharpness / creaminess |
| **Pickles** | Turnip, Cucumber, Chili | Essential acidity and crunch |
| **Vegetables** | Tomato, Cucumber, Onion, Parsley, Lettuce | Bright, fresh contrast |
| **Condiments** | Sumac, Hot sauce | Sprinkle for tang and heat |
| **Sides** | Hummus, Tabbouleh, Fattoush, Fries | Common plate accompaniments |

---

**Notes:**  
- Marinate at least 12 hours for best flavor.  
- Maintain steady rotisserie temperature and baste frequently.  
- Let meat rest before slicing to retain juices.
