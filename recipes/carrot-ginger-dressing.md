# Carrot Ginger Dressing

**Source:** [Just One Cookbook](https://www.justonecookbook.com/carrot-ginger-dressing/)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 0 minutes
**Yield:** 6 servings (roughly 1½ cups per batch)

If you enjoy the sweet-tangy salad dressing served at Japanese restaurants, this delicious Carrot Ginger Dressing recipe is for you. Seasoned with miso, rice vinegar, and sesame oil, it's a light and refreshing dressing that will zest up any green salad.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 carrot (4.8 oz, 135 g)
- ¼ onion (2.5 oz, 70 g)
- 1 knob ginger (1 inch, 2.5 cm; 0.6 oz, 17 g; preferably milder young/spring ginger)
- 1½ Tbsp sugar
- 1 Tbsp miso (mild and mellow white miso)
- ¼ tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt (to taste)
- freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
- ½ cup rice vinegar (unseasoned; DO NOT substitute other vinegars as they are too acidic and strong)
- ¼ cup neutral oil

---

## Instructions

### To Prep the Vegetables

1. **Gather:** Collect all the ingredients.

2. **Chop the carrot:** Peel 1 carrot and chop it into pieces about ½–1 inch (1.25–2.5 cm).

3. **Chop the onion:** Chop ¼ onion into 1-inch (2.5-cm) pieces.

4. **Prep the ginger:** Peel 1 knob ginger and slice into small pieces. Put all the chopped vegetables into a food processor or a high-powered home blender like Vitamix.

### To Blend the Dressing

5. **Purée the vegetables:** Purée until fine and smooth.

6. **Add seasonings:** Add 1½ Tbsp sugar, 1 Tbsp miso, ¼ tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt, and freshly ground black pepper to the vegetable purée.

7. **Add liquids:** Add 1 tsp toasted sesame oil, ½ cup rice vinegar (unseasoned), and ¼ cup neutral oil.

8. **Final blend:** Process again until the dressing is completely smooth. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt, as needed. If it's too sour, add a bit more sugar. If you prefer a lighter dressing, add water to thin it; don't add water if you want the dressing to keep longer.

### To Serve

9. **Serve:** Serve over a green salad, such as iceberg lettuce, cucumber, red radishes, and tomatoes.

---

## Storage

- Store in an airtight container or mason jar for up to 1 week. Shake well before using.
- If you take out the portion you need and return the dressing to the refrigerator right away, it will keep for up to 2 weeks.
- You can also store it in the freezer for up to a month.
