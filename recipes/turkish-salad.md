# Turkish Salad

**Yield:** 6–8 servings

A simple, vibrant Middle Eastern salad of shredded cabbage, romaine, cucumber, olives, and tomatoes dressed with olive oil and lemon juice. Best dressed just before serving to keep it crisp and fresh.

---

## Ingredients

- 5½ oz (150 g) white cabbage, finely shredded
- 3½ oz (100 g) romaine lettuce, finely shredded
- ½ cup (50 g) fresh cilantro leaves, finely chopped
- 2 small Turkish/Middle Eastern cucumbers or 5½ oz (150 g) English cucumber
- 2 large scallions, finely sliced
- 2¾ oz (75 g) pitted dry black olives
- 1 tbsp dried mint
- 2 large ripe plum tomatoes (5½ oz/150 g), roughly chopped
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- ¾ tsp sea salt flakes

---

## Instructions

1. **Combine the base:** Put the shredded cabbage, lettuce, and cilantro into a large bowl.

2. **Prepare the cucumbers:** Peel 2–3 vertical strips of skin off the cucumbers, then cut them in half lengthways, then lengthways again, and chop into ¼ inch (0.5 cm) thick pieces. Add the cucumbers to the bowl with the scallions, olives, and dried mint. Add the tomatoes, but keep them slightly separate from the other ingredients. Do not stir.

3. **Dress and serve:** Just before serving, pour over the extra virgin olive oil and lemon juice. Sprinkle over the salt, give everything a gentle stir, and serve immediately. If you are making the salad in advance, then store in the fridge and dress just before serving to keep it as fresh as possible.
