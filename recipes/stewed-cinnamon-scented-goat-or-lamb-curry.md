# Stewed Cinnamon-Scented Goat or Lamb Curry

**Source:** Elegant & Inspired Vij's Indian Cuisine Cookbook (via The Dirty Apron)

**Yield:** 6 servings

A slow-stewed Vij's curry with goat or lamb, fragrant with whole cloves and cinnamon, built on a deeply caramelised onion-tomato-yogurt masala and cooked low and slow until moist and thick.

---

## Ingredients

- 4 to 5 tbsp ghee (or canola oil)
- 1 tbsp cumin seeds
- 2 large onions, chopped
- 7 large cloves garlic, chopped (about 1oz.)
- 1 tbsp finely chopped or lightly crushed ginger
- 2 tbsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 10 cloves
- 1 cinnamon stick (2 inches long — add another 1 stick if you prefer stronger taste)
- 1 tsp ground cayenne pepper
- 1 tbsp salt
- 5 ripe tomatoes, chopped (about ½ lbs.)
- 1 cup plain yogurt (stirred)
- 1 cup water
- ¼ cup canola oil
- 2 lbs leg of lamb (or goat, fat trimmed, cut in 1½ inch cubes)
- ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro

---

## Method

1. **Build the masala:** Melt ghee (or 4 tbsp of oil) on medium heat in a large, heavy stockpot. Add cumin seeds and sauté until they sizzle, about 45 seconds. Add onions and sauté until golden brown, about 8 to 10 minutes. Add garlic and sauté 2 to 3 minutes, or until golden brown. Stir in ginger. After 1 minute, add ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, cloves, cinnamon, cayenne and salt. Cook on medium heat, stirring regularly, for 5 to 10 minutes or until ghee (or oil) separates from the spices. Add another tablespoon of ghee or oil if spices are sticking to the bottom of the pot.

2. **Add tomatoes and yogurt:** Add tomatoes and cook for 3 to 4 minutes, until ghee (or oil) separates again and glistens. Stir in yogurt and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes, then add water (add an extra cup of water if serving rice). Bring to a boil, then remove from heat.

3. **Sear the meat:** In another large, heavy frying pan, add ¼ cup oil (make sure there is enough to lightly cover the bottom of the pan). On medium to high heat, sauté lamb (or goat), stirring regularly, until you notice small, thin lines of blood on the meat. Remove from the heat and transfer meat to the stew.

4. **Slow cook:** Return stew to medium-low heat and cook covered, stirring occasionally, for 2 hours or until meat is cooked through. Add more water, ½ cup at a time, if the stew becomes dry while cooking. This should be a moist thick curry.

---

## To Serve

Just before serving, remove cinnamon stick and cloves. Stir in cilantro. Ladle equal amounts of curry into each bowl.
