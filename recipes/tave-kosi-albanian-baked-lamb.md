# Tavë Kosi – Albanian Baked Lamb with Rice and Yogurt Custard

**Serves:** 6–8  
**Prep Time:** 20 min  
**Cook Time:** 1 hr 20 min  
**Total Time:** ~1 hr 40 min

---

## Ingredients

### For the lamb:
- **1.2 kg** lamb shoulder or leg, cut into chunks
- **1½ tbsp** butter or olive oil
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1½ tsp** salt
- **¾ tsp** black pepper
- **500 ml** water (for pressure cooking)

### For the rice:
- **¾ cup** short-grain or medium-grain rice (e.g. Arborio or Calrose)
- **200 ml** reserved lamb cooking liquid
- **1 tbsp** butter or olive oil
- **1 garlic clove**, minced (optional)
- Salt to taste

### For the roux:
- **3 tbsp (45 g)** butter
- **3 tbsp** all-purpose flour
- **150 ml** reserved lamb cooking liquid

### For the yogurt custard:
- **750 ml** full-fat plain yogurt (3 cups)
- **3 large eggs**
- **66 ml** egg whites (about 4½ tbsp, or whites from 2 large eggs)
- **⅜ tsp** ground nutmeg (optional)
- Salt to taste

---

## Instructions

### 1. Cook the lamb
- In a deep skillet or pressure cooker, heat **1½ tbsp butter or oil** over medium-high.
- Brown lamb chunks on all sides (7–8 minutes).
- Add garlic, salt, and pepper.
- **Pressure cooker:** Add **500 ml water**, seal, and cook on high pressure for **25 minutes**. Let pressure release naturally.
- Remove lamb and **reserve all cooking liquid** (you need ~350 ml total — top up with water if short).

### 2. Par-cook the rice
- In a saucepan, melt **1 tbsp butter or oil** over medium heat. Sauté garlic briefly if using.
- Stir in rice and toast for 1 minute.
- Add **200 ml reserved lamb cooking liquid** and a pinch of salt. Bring to a gentle simmer.
- Simmer uncovered for **8–10 minutes** until partially cooked and liquid is mostly absorbed. It will finish in the oven.

### 3. Make the roux
- Melt **3 tbsp butter** in a saucepan over medium heat.
- Stir in **3 tbsp flour** and cook for 1–2 minutes until pale gold.
- Slowly whisk in **150 ml reserved cooking liquid** to form a smooth, slightly thickened sauce.
- Let cool slightly.

### 4. Make the yogurt custard
- In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, egg whites, and yogurt.
- Stir in the cooled roux, nutmeg (if using), and salt to taste. Mix well.

### 5. Assemble and bake
- Preheat oven to **375°F (190°C)**. Lightly butter a **9x13-inch** baking dish.
- Spread lamb evenly in the dish.
- Spoon par-cooked rice over and around the lamb.
- Pour yogurt custard evenly over the top.
- Bake uncovered for **50–60 minutes**, until the custard is set and golden on top.
- Rest **10–15 minutes** before serving.
