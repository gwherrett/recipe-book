# Suya-Spiced Nuts

**Total Time:** 20 minutes
**Yield:** Serves 10

Drawing inspiration from chef Simileoluwa Adebajo's Chicken Suya recipe, F&W Associate Editorial Director Chandra Ram coats mixed nuts with butter and suya spice for a Nigerian-inspired riff on a popular bar snack that's hot, sweet, and salty.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 (0.2-oz.) chicken bouillon cube
- 1 Tbsp. ground ginger
- 1 Tbsp. paprika
- 2 tsp. garlic powder
- 2 tsp. onion powder
- ½ tsp. black pepper
- ½ tsp. cayenne pepper
- 1 Tbsp. unsalted butter
- 5 cups salted mixed nuts
- 1 Tbsp. light brown sugar

---

## Instructions

1. **Toast the spice blend:** Combine bouillon cube, ginger, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a spice grinder; pulse until finely ground, about 20 pulses. Transfer spice mixture to a large skillet; cook over medium-low, shaking skillet often, until mixture is fragrant and toasted, about 4 minutes.

2. **Toast the nuts:** Transfer toasted spice mixture to a large heatproof bowl; let cool to room temperature, about 10 minutes. Do not wipe skillet clean. Add butter to skillet, and melt over medium. Add nuts; cook, stirring often, until lightly toasted, about 8 minutes.

3. **Combine and serve:** Add nuts to spice mixture in bowl. Add brown sugar, and toss to combine. Serve immediately. Alternatively, spread nuts on a large rimmed baking sheet, and let cool to room temperature, about 30 minutes. —CHANDRA RAM

---

## Make Ahead

Cooled nut mixture can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature up to 1 week.
