# Pork Tenderloin a la Normande

**Yield:** 4 servings

---

## Ingredients

- 1½ pounds pork tenderloin, cut in ¾-inch pieces and flattened
- Salt and pepper
- 3 tablespoons butter
- ¼ cup shallot, finely chopped
- 3 tablespoons Calvados
- ½ cup dry white wine
- 1½ cups cream
- 1/8 teaspoon thyme, dried, crushed
- 1/8 teaspoon tarragon, dried, crushed
- 2 apples, cut in 8 slices
- 2 tablespoons parsley, chopped

---

## Instructions

1. **Sear the pork:** Heat 2 tablespoons butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sauté pork medallions in a single layer (do this in batches if necessary) until lightly browned on both sides. Lower the heat if necessary to keep the butter from burning.

2. **Flambé and set aside:** Flambé with 2 tablespoons of Calvados. Remove pork from skillet as it is done and set aside.

3. **Make the sauce:** Melt 1 tablespoon butter in the same skillet. Add shallot and cook until tender, stirring occasionally. Add wine and simmer until the mixture is reduced by one-half. Add cream and simmer until sauce is thick enough to coat a spoon. Strain sauce and return to the pan, then add thyme and tarragon. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Add pork medallions and simmer for 4–5 minutes longer.

4. **Finish with apples:** In a separate pan, sauté apple slices in the remaining 1 tablespoon butter. Flambé with 1 tablespoon of Calvados. Add some of the sauce and continue cooking for 1 minute. Put slices over pork medallions and garnish with parsley.
