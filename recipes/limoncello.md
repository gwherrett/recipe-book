# Limoncello

**Source:** [Antonella — Sicilian Cook in Tuscany](https://www.antonella.cooking/recipe/how-to-make-a-perfect-limoncello/)

**Prep Time:** 2 hours (plus 15 days infusion)
**Yield:** ~1.5 litres

Craft your own exquisite limoncello at home with just three ingredients. Dive into the essence of Sicilian lemons and savor the taste of summer.

---

## Ingredients

- 6 organic lemons
- 500 ml grain alcohol
- 550 ml water
- 400 g sugar

---

## Instructions

1. **Zest the lemons:** Wash the lemons and dry well. Cut out the zest using a peeler. Take just the yellow part — the white pith is bitter.

2. **Infuse:** Put the zest in a jar and add the alcohol. Close the lid and place the jar in a dark place for a minimum of 15 days. Shake the jar every day if you remember.

3. **Make the syrup:** On the day you prepare the limoncello, place the water and sugar in a heavy pan on a low flame. Bring to the boil and cook until the sugar is melted. Remove from the flame and let it cool completely.

4. **Combine:** Strain the infused alcohol through a fine strainer to remove the lemon zest, then add it to the cooled syrup. Stir to combine.

5. **Bottle:** Pour into clean, sterilised bottles.

---

## Notes

- Get the best lemons you can find with a good thick zest. Use organic lemons — pesticides remain on the skin and the alcohol will absorb them during infusion.
- Don't waste your peeled lemons — store some in the fridge for a few days or squeeze the rest and freeze the juice in an ice cube tray.
- For a stronger taste, use the same amount of water as alcohol (1:1 ratio).
- Store one bottle in the fridge and the rest in a dry, cool place.
- For a tarter, less sweet limoncello, reduce the sugar to 250–300 g. You can also add a small amount of freshly squeezed lemon juice to the finished batch for extra citric sharpness.
- Chill the glasses in the freezer for 30 minutes before serving.
