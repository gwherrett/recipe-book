# Japanese Slaw with Brussels Sprouts

## Ingredients

### For the Slaw:
- 1 lb Brussels sprouts, thinly sliced  
- 1 large carrot, julienned  
- 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced  
- 2 green onions, thinly sliced  
- 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped  

### For the Dressing:
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce  
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar  
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil  
- 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup  
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated  
- 1 clove garlic, minced  
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)  
- 1 teaspoon sriracha (optional, for heat)  

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Vegetables:**  
   Trim the ends of the Brussels sprouts and remove any discolored outer leaves. Thinly slice them using a sharp knife or a mandoline.  
   Julienne the carrot.  
   Thinly slice the red bell pepper and green onions.  
   Chop the cilantro.  

2. **Make the Dressing:**  
   In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey (or maple syrup), grated ginger, and minced garlic. Add sesame seeds and sriracha if using.  

3. **Combine:**  
   In a large bowl, combine the sliced Brussels sprouts, julienned carrot, sliced red bell pepper, green onions, and chopped cilantro.  
   Pour the dressing over the vegetables and toss to combine.  

4. **Serve:**  
   Let the slaw sit for about 10â€“15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.  
   Serve as a side dish or as a topping for sandwiches or tacos.  

Enjoy your Japanese-inspired Brussels sprouts slaw!
