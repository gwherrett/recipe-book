# Salt, Pepper, and Lime Dipping Sauce (Muối Tiêu Chanh)

## Category
Condiments & Sauces → Vietnamese

## Summary
A simple, pungent, bright Vietnamese dipping sauce made from coarse salt, white pepper, lime, and optional sliced chilies. Commonly served with grilled meats, seafood, shaking beef, and poached chicken. Diners mix their own proportions at the table.

## Ingredients
- Kosher or other coarse salt  
- White pepper  
- Lime wedges  
- Thinly sliced Thai or serrano chiles (optional)

## Method
1. Set out shallow dishes of each ingredient on the table.  
2. In individual dipping dishes, diners combine:  
   - 2 parts salt to 1 part white pepper  
   - A squeeze of lime juice  
   - (Optional) Muddle chile slices with chopsticks to release their oils  
3. Dip each bite of food into the sauce and adjust proportions as desired throughout the meal.

## Alternative Assembly
- Pre‑portion small mounds of salt and white pepper in each diner’s dish and let them add lime and chili to taste.
