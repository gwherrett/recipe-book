## Ingredients

### Slaw
- 3 cups green cabbage, finely shredded
- 2 cups brussels sprouts, trimmed and very thinly sliced (shaved)
- 1 cup carrots, grated
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt (for drawing out moisture)

### Dressing
- ⅓ cup buttermilk
- ⅓ cup mayonnaise
- 1½ tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 1 tablespoon sugar (adjust to taste)
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- ½ teaspoon celery seed (optional)


## Method

1. **Salt and drain the cabbage** – Toss the shredded cabbage and shaved brussels sprouts with 1 tsp kosher salt in a large colander. Let sit for 20–30 minutes — the salt will draw out excess water. Squeeze firmly by the handful (or press in a clean towel) to remove as much liquid as possible, then transfer to a large bowl with the grated carrots.
2. **Make the dressing** – Whisk together the buttermilk, mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, sugar, black pepper, and celery seed (if using) until smooth. Taste and adjust — add more sugar for sweetness or vinegar for tang.
3. **Combine** – Pour the dressing over the vegetables and toss well to coat evenly.
4. **Chill** – Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let the flavours meld. The slaw will stay crisp because the excess water was removed upfront.
5. **Serve** – Give it a final toss before serving. Great alongside BBQ, fried chicken, or in sandwiches.
