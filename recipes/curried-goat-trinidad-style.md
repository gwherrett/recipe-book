# Caribbean Goat Curry

**Source:** Merged from *Recipes from Jamaica* — Jill Roberts, and traditional Trinidadian recipe

**Yield:** 6 servings

A Jamaican-style curried goat using a Trinidadian spice blend — individual ground spices rather than pre-made curry powder — slow-simmered until tender and finished with coconut milk and butter.

---

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds goat meat

### Spice mix (Trinidadian, scaled to 2 lb)

- 1 tablespoon ground coriander
- 1½ teaspoons turmeric
- 1 tablespoon red chile powder (powdered pure chiles, without added spices)
- 1½ teaspoons ground cumin
- ¾ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

### Aromatics & base

- ½ cup onions, sliced
- 1 stalk scallion
- 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 1-inch piece ginger, finely chopped
- 1 hot pepper, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 teaspoon lime juice
- 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil

### To finish

- 6 cups boiling water (add more as needed)
- ½ cup coconut milk
- 2 ounces butter
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 sprig thyme

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate the meat:** Wash goat meat and cut into pieces. Combine all spice mix ingredients and rub over the meat. Add scallion, onion, garlic, ginger, and hot pepper and work everything into the meat. Sprinkle with salt. Cover and let stand for 1½ hours.

2. **Brown and simmer:** Heat the ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add the meat and all marinade, sprinkle lime juice over, and fry lightly for five minutes, stirring. Add 4½ cups boiling water and simmer, covered, 2–3 hours until meat is tender, stirring from time to time. Add more water as needed.

3. **Finish:** Add coconut milk, butter, sugar, and thyme. Cook for five minutes more.

4. **Serve:** Serve with plain white rice, roti, chutney, sliced beets, grated coconut, raisins, ripe banana, and sliced sweet peppers.
