# Fattoush-Style Green Cabbage Slaw

**Yield:** 4–6 servings

A Middle Eastern-inspired slaw that borrows all the hallmark flavours of fattoush — sumac, lemon, mint, parsley, radishes — but built on shredded green cabbage with a tangy buttermilk dressing instead of a bread salad base. The buttermilk keeps the dressing light and pourable rather than gloppy.

---

## Ingredients

### Slaw

- ½ small green cabbage (~450g / 1 lb), quartered and very finely shredded
- 6 radishes, thinly sliced into rounds
- 1 small Lebanese cucumber (or ½ English cucumber), halved lengthways and thinly sliced
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced on the diagonal
- 1 large handful flat-leaf parsley leaves, roughly chopped
- 1 large handful fresh mint leaves, torn

### Buttermilk Sumac Dressing

- 120 ml (½ cup) buttermilk
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1½ tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1½ tsp sumac, plus extra to finish
- 1 small garlic clove, finely grated or minced
- ½ tsp fine salt
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp pomegranate molasses (optional but recommended — adds a fruity depth)

---

## Method

1. **Salt the cabbage:** Put the shredded cabbage in a large bowl. Sprinkle with ¼ tsp salt, toss well, and leave to stand for 10–15 minutes. This softens the cabbage slightly and draws out excess water. Drain off any liquid that collects.

2. **Make the dressing:** Whisk together all the dressing ingredients. Taste and adjust — it should be noticeably tart and well-seasoned.

3. **Dress the cabbage:** Pour the dressing over the cabbage and toss thoroughly. Leave to sit for at least 5 minutes so the cabbage absorbs the flavours.

4. **Add the vegetables:** Add the radishes, cucumber, and spring onions. Toss again.

5. **Finish and serve:** Just before serving, fold in the parsley and mint. Transfer to a serving platter or bowl, and finish with an extra pinch of sumac over the top.

---

## Notes

- The slaw holds reasonably well for a few hours in the fridge (the cabbage softens further), but add the herbs only just before serving.
- Great alongside grilled chicken, lamb kofta, falafel, or stuffed into a wrap.
- For crunch and to lean fully into the fattoush spirit, scatter over a handful of toasted or fried pita pieces just before serving.
