# Salted Chocolate Chip Cookies

**Source:** Boyle's Bakers!

**Yield:** approximately 1 dozen (12 cookies)

Classic chocolate chip cookies finished with flaky sea salt — crisp edges, soft centres, and that perfect sweet-salty balance.

---

## Ingredients

- ½ cup unsalted butter, room temperature
- ½ cup brown sugar
- ¼ cup granulated white sugar
- 1 large egg, room temperature
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- ¼ tsp baking soda
- ¼ tsp baking powder
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 cup chocolate chips
- Flaky sea salt, for finishing

---

## Instructions

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.

1. **Mix the dry ingredients:** In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder and salt. Set aside.

2. **Cream the butter and sugars:** Using a handheld mixer, beat together the butter and sugars in a medium bowl on high speed until creamy and incorporated, around two minutes.

3. **Add egg and vanilla:** Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat on medium speed until incorporated.

4. **Combine wet and dry:** Add the flour mixture to the butter and sugar mixture. Mix together on low speed until incorporated, about one minute.

5. **Fold in chips:** Fold in the chocolate chips.

6. **Portion the dough:** Using a large spoon and clean hands, scoop out even portions of dough and gently roll into a ball. Place the dough balls on the prepared baking pan with two inches of space between.

7. **Top with extra chips:** Add a few extra chocolate chips to the top of each dough ball.

8. **Bake:** Bake in the oven for 13–15 minutes until lightly golden with crisp edges. Using oven mitts, carefully remove the pan from the oven.

---

## Cookie Hacks

- Using oven mitts, lightly rap the sheet pan to help flatten and spread the cookies. Alternatively, use an offset spatula to lightly flatten the cookie.
- Using a large round cookie cutter, "scoot" the cookie into the perfect round shape, if desired.
- Finish each cookie with a few flakes of flaky salt (do NOT overdo it with flaky salt or you will ruin your cookies, which would be very sad!)
