# Khoresh Aloo (Persian Prune & Split Pea Stew)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 2 hours 15 minutes
**Yield:** 4 servings

Classic Persian slow-braised lamb stew with prunes, yellow split peas, saffron, and date syrup. The split peas add body and earthiness; date syrup provides the traditional sweet-sour balance alongside lemon juice.

---

## Ingredients

- 700g (1.5 lb) lamb shoulder or shank, cut into 4–5cm chunks
- 1 large onion, thinly sliced
- ½ cup yellow split peas, rinsed
- 1 cup pitted prunes (or aloo bokhara / dried yellow plums)
- 2 tbsp date syrup (dibs)
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice (adjust to taste)
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- ½ tsp salt, plus more to taste
- ¼ tsp saffron threads, bloomed in 3 tbsp hot water
- 2 tbsp ghee or neutral oil
- 1½ cups water or light stock

---

## Instructions

1. **Brown the lamb:** Heat ghee in a heavy pot over medium-high. Brown the lamb in batches until deep golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.

2. **Sauté the onion:** Reduce heat to medium. In the same pot, sauté the onion until soft and golden, about 10 minutes.

3. **Add spices:** Add turmeric, cinnamon, and black pepper; stir for 1 minute.

4. **Braise the lamb:** Return the lamb to the pot. Add water or stock and bring to a boil. Skim any foam. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.

5. **Add split peas:** Add the rinsed split peas. Cover and cook for a further 30 minutes until the peas are just tender but not falling apart.

6. **Add prunes and sweetener:** Add the prunes, date syrup, and lemon juice. Stir gently, cover, and simmer on low for another 30–40 minutes until the lamb is very tender and the stew has thickened.

7. **Finish with saffron:** Stir in the bloomed saffron. Taste and adjust — add more lemon juice for tartness or more date syrup for sweetness. Season with salt.

8. **Rest before serving:** Rest off the heat for 10 minutes before serving (ja-oftadan — allows the oil to settle and flavours to meld).

Serve over saffron basmati rice (chelo). Accompaniments: plain yogurt, fresh herbs (tarragon, mint), flatbread.

---

## Notes

- Aloo bokhara (dried golden plums) are more traditional than prunes and are tarter — reduce lemon juice slightly if using them.
- Date syrup (dibs) is the authentic Persian sweetener here; pomegranate molasses is a common alternative but changes the flavour profile significantly.
- The split peas should hold their shape; if they start to dissolve, the stew gains a lovely thick texture — both results are acceptable.
- Can be made a day ahead; the flavour deepens overnight.
