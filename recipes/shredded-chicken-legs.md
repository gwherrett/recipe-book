# Pressure-Cooked Shredded Chicken Legs (Mexican Flavours)

**Category:** Mains → Stews & Shredded Meats  
**Tags:** Instant Pot, Chicken, Tacos, Meal Prep  
**Yield:** ~6–8 servings (about 1.2–1.4 kg shredded meat & sauce)  
**Active Time:** 15 minutes • **Pressure Time:** 15 minutes + 10 min natural release • **Total:** ~40 minutes

## Ingredients
- 1.3–1.6 kg bone‑in, skin‑on chicken legs (thighs/drumsticks) — skin removed
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (optional, for sauté)
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 jalapeño or serrano, minced (optional)
- 400 g can diced tomatoes (14 oz), with juices
- 240 ml low‑sodium chicken stock (1 cup)
- 1–2 tbsp adobo from canned chipotles **or** 1 chipotle in adobo, minced
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar **or** lime juice (plus more to taste)

### Spice mix
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp dried oregano (Mexican if possible)
- 1½ tsp smoked paprika (or 1 tsp regular paprika + ½ tsp ancho powder)
- 1 tsp ground coriander (optional)
- 1 tsp chili powder (US‑style blend) **or** extra ancho/guajillo to taste
- 1 tsp kosher salt (Diamond Crystal; use ½ tsp if table salt), plus more to taste
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 bay leaf

## Method
1. **Season.** Pat chicken dry; toss with the spice mix (reserve the bay leaf).  
2. **Sauté aromatics (optional but tasty).** On *Sauté* (Normal), heat oil. Cook onion 3–4 min until translucent; add garlic & jalapeño 30–60 sec until fragrant.  
3. **Load the pot.** Add tomatoes, stock, chipotle/adobo, tomato paste, vinegar/lime. Stir, then add chicken and the bay leaf, submerging pieces.  
4. **Pressure cook.** Seal and cook **High Pressure 15 minutes**. Let **natural release 10 minutes**, then quick‑release remaining pressure.  
5. **Shred.** Transfer chicken to a tray; discard bones. Shred with two forks. Skim excess fat from the pot if desired.  
6. **Reduce & finish.** Switch back to *Sauté*. Simmer sauce 5–8 min to thicken slightly. Return shredded chicken to the pot; toss to coat. Adjust salt and acidity with more salt and lime to brighten.  
7. **Serve.** Rest 5 minutes to absorb sauce.

## Uses
- Tacos, tostadas, burritos, quesadillas, tortas, taco salads, nachos.
- Breakfast: skillet hash with potatoes & eggs, or chilaquiles.

## Tips & Variations
- **Tinga‑style:** Add ½ thinly sliced white onion at the end and ½ tsp dried thyme.  
- **Citrus note:** Add 60 ml (¼ cup) orange juice with the liquids for a carnitas‑like aroma.  
- **Spicier:** Add extra chipotle or ¼ tsp cayenne.  
- **No sauté option:** Skip Step 2; still delicious.  
- **Skin on?** Remove and discard after cooking to keep the sauce from getting greasy.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 4 days or freeze up to 3 months. Reheat gently with a splash of stock or water.
