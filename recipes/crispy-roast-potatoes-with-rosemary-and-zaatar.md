# Crispy Roast Potatoes with Rosemary and Za'atar

**Source:** [Yotam Ottolenghi's Crowd Pleaser, Menu 1: The Full Roast Dinner](https://www.channel4.com/programmes/ottolenghis-crowd-pleasers)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 1 hour 40 minutes
**Yield:** 6–8 servings (as a side)

Classic British roasties with a Middle Eastern twist — par-boiled Maris Pipers roasted in hot sunflower oil until deeply golden, then finished with fragrant rosemary and za'atar.

---

## Ingredients

- 3 large rosemary sprigs (2 left whole, 1 picked and finely chopped)
- 3 bay leaves
- 10 garlic cloves, skin on
- 2.5kg Maris Piper potatoes, peeled and cut into 4–5cm chunks
- 150ml sunflower oil
- 2 tbsp za'atar
- Fine sea salt and flaked sea salt

---

## Instructions

1. **Par-boil:** Add 2 tablespoons of fine sea salt, 2 rosemary sprigs, the bay leaves, and garlic cloves to a pot of cold water. Add the potatoes and bring to the boil over high heat. Lower to medium and cook for 15–18 minutes, or until easily pierced with a knife. Drain well through a colander and let steam-dry for 15 minutes. Set the garlic cloves aside.

2. **Preheat:** Heat the oven to 220°C.

3. **Heat the oil:** Add the oil to a large 30cm × 40cm roasting tray (or divide between two medium tins) and place in the oven to heat up, about 10 minutes.

4. **First roast:** Carefully add the potatoes to the hot oil, taking care not to splash yourself. Spread out with a spatula — they should not be stacked. Roast for 25 minutes, then gently turn over with a spatula. Roast for another 30–35 minutes, adding the reserved garlic cloves in the last 10 minutes, until deeply golden and crispy.

5. **Finish with za'atar:** Combine the finely chopped rosemary, za'atar, and ¼ teaspoon of flaked sea salt in a small bowl. Scatter over the potatoes and return to the oven for 2 minutes more, until fragrant. Serve hot.
