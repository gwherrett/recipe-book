# Barbancourt Chicken

**Yield:** 4 servings

A Haitian-style marinated chicken braised in a citrus and rum marinade, finished with a blended pan sauce and garnished with orange slices and toasted almonds.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 2½-pound fryer, cut into pieces

### Marinade

- 3 green onions with part of the stem, cut into large pieces
- 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley
- ⅛ teaspoon of hot red pepper
- ½ cup of fresh orange juice
- 6 tablespoons of Rhum Barbancourt
- ¼ cup of fresh lime juice
- 4 peeled garlic buds
- 1 level teaspoon of thyme
- 1 level teaspoon of salt
- ¼ teaspoon of freshly ground pepper

### To Finish

- Flour
- About 3 tablespoons of butter
- Thinly sliced peeled oranges
- Fresh chopped parsley and sliced toasted almonds

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate:** Rinse chicken. Dry well. Combine the first ten ingredients and mix till smooth. Marinate the chicken pieces in the mixture for 1 day.

2. **Brown the chicken:** Drain chicken pieces, squeeze, and dry. Keep the marinade for later. Brown the chicken in a thick-bottomed pan with a little butter.

3. **Simmer:** Add the rest of the marinade, cover, and let simmer around 30 minutes, turning over once, until the chicken is tender.

4. **Make the sauce:** Place the chicken in a serving dish. Run the cooking juices through a blender then return to the range to reduce it slightly. Pour over the chicken.

5. **Garnish and serve:** Decorate with slices of orange and sprinkle with chopped parsley and grilled almonds.
