# Roasted Hispi Cabbage with Pine Nut Vinaigrette

**Source:** [Yotam Ottolenghi's Crowd Pleaser, Menu 1: The Full Roast Dinner](https://www.channel4.com/programmes/ottolenghis-crowd-pleasers)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25 minutes
**Yield:** 4–6 servings (as a side)

Hispi (pointed) cabbage roasted at high heat until charred and tender, tossed with fresh basil and a warm toasted pine nut vinaigrette with grapefruit and Dijon.

---

## Ingredients

- 2 hispi cabbages (1kg), cut into quarters lengthways, core removed
- 100ml cold-pressed rapeseed oil (or olive oil)
- 20g pine nuts
- 40ml white grapefruit juice (or lemon juice)
- ½ small shallot, finely chopped (15g)
- ½ tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp honey or maple syrup
- 30g picked basil leaves
- Fine sea salt and black pepper

---

## Instructions

1. **Preheat the oven** to 240°C.

2. **Roast the cabbage:** Place the cabbage quarters in a large, high-sided tray with 50ml of the oil, 1½ teaspoons of fine sea salt, and plenty of black pepper. Mix well and roast, stirring halfway through, for 15–20 minutes, until softened and charred in places.

3. **Make the vinaigrette:** Toast the pine nuts in a small frying pan over medium heat for about 5 minutes, shaking occasionally, until golden. Whisk together the remaining 50ml of oil with the grapefruit juice, shallot, mustard, honey, ¼ teaspoon of salt, and a generous grind of pepper. Finely chop the warm pine nuts and whisk into the dressing.

4. **Finish and serve:** Stir the basil through the roasted hispi, pour over the dressing, and serve.
