# Cantonese Steamed Egg with Chinese Doughnuts

**Cook Time:** 10–15 minutes
**Yield:** 2–3 servings

A silky Cantonese steamed egg custard poured over pieces of youtiao (Chinese doughnut), steamed until just set and garnished with scallions and a drizzle of soy sauce.

---

## Ingredients

- 3 large eggs
- ½ cup water or broth (chicken broth recommended)
- 1 Chinese doughnut (youtiao), cut into 1-inch pieces
- ½ teaspoon soy sauce
- ¼ teaspoon white pepper
- ½ teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
- 1 teaspoon Shaoxing wine (optional)
- Chopped scallions or cilantro (for garnish)
- A drizzle of light soy sauce (for serving)
- Fried shallots (optional, for garnish)

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Youtiao:** Cut the Chinese doughnut into 1-inch pieces. If the youtiao is stale, toast it in a dry pan or oven at 350°F (180°C) for 3–5 minutes. Arrange the pieces in a heatproof shallow dish for steaming.

2. **Make the Egg Mixture:** Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl. Add water or broth, soy sauce, white pepper, sesame oil, and Shaoxing wine (if using). Beat well until the mixture is smooth and uniform. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve (optional) for a silkier texture.

3. **Steam the Dish:** Pour the egg mixture over the youtiao in the dish. Cover the dish lightly with foil or a heatproof lid to prevent water droplets from falling onto the eggs. Place the dish in a steamer and steam over medium-low heat for 10–15 minutes until the egg is just set.

4. **Garnish & Serve:** Remove from the steamer and let it rest for 1–2 minutes. Garnish with chopped scallions, a drizzle of light soy sauce, and fried shallots (if using). Serve immediately with congee or rice.

---

## Tips for Success

- Use a 1:2 egg-to-liquid ratio for the best silky texture.
- Steam gently to avoid bubbles and a rough texture.
- Strain the egg mixture for ultra-smooth results.
- For a firmer texture, reduce the water slightly.
