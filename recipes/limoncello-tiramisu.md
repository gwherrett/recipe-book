# Limoncello Tiramisu

**Time:** 25 min active, 4–12 hr chill
**Serves:** 12

Note: This recipe uses uncooked eggs. If salmonella is a concern, use pasteurized in-shell eggs.

## Summary
A bright, lemony twist on the classic tiramisu. Ladyfingers are dipped in a limoncello-milk soak and layered with a soft mascarpone cream balanced with lemon juice and zest. Perfect make-ahead dessert.

## Ingredients

**Soak**
- 1 cup (240 ml) milk
- 1 cup (240 ml) limoncello

**Cream**
- 3 large egg yolks
- 1/2 cup (100 g) sugar, divided
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tbsp grated lemon zest (yellow only)
- 1 lb (450 g) mascarpone cheese, cold
- 2 cups (480 ml) heavy cream, cold

**Assembly & Finish**
- ~5 dozen ladyfinger cookies (savoiardi)
- 3 egg whites
- Finely grated lemon zest, for garnish

## Method

### 1) Make the soak
Whisk together the **milk** and **limoncello** in a shallow bowl. Set aside.

### 2) Make the cream
In a stand mixer or with a hand mixer, beat **egg yolks**, **1/4 cup (50 g) sugar**, **lemon juice**, and **lemon zest** until pale and light, 3–5 minutes. Add **mascarpone** and **heavy cream**; beat until fluffy.

In a clean bowl, whip **egg whites** with the remaining **1/4 cup (50 g) sugar** to stiff peaks. Gently fold into the mascarpone mixture.

### 3) Assemble
Quickly dip each ladyfinger in the **soak** (do not soak through) and arrange a single layer in a 13×9-inch (33×23 cm) dish. Spread half the **cream** over top. Repeat with a second layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining cream.

### 4) Chill
Cover and refrigerate for **at least 4 hours** (overnight recommended) to set and meld flavors.

### 5) Serve
Garnish generously with **lemon zest**. Slice and serve cold.

## Notes & Tips
- **Make-ahead:** Keeps 2–3 days refrigerated; flavor improves by day 2.
- **Lemon balance:** The lemon juice in the cream adds brightness; taste the cream before assembling and adjust sugar if needed.
