# Tiramisu

**Time:** 30 min active, 6–24 hr chill
**Serves:** 12

Note: This recipe uses uncooked eggs. If salmonella is a concern, use pasteurized in-shell eggs or cook yolks as a sabayon (see method).

## Summary
The classic Italian no-bake dessert. Coffee-soaked ladyfingers layered with a rich, airy mascarpone cream, finished with a dusting of cocoa.

## Ingredients

**Coffee soak**
- 1½–2 cups (350–475 ml) strong espresso or cold brew, cooled
- 2–4 tbsp Marsala, rum, or coffee liqueur (optional)
- 1 tbsp sugar (optional)

**Mascarpone cream**
- 4 large egg yolks
- 3 large egg whites (or 300 ml / 1¼ cups heavy cream, whipped — see method)
- 90–120 g (½ cup) sugar, to taste
- 500 g (1 lb) mascarpone, cold but pliable

**Assembly & finish**
- 40–48 ladyfinger cookies / savoiardi (~400–450 g, about 2 sleeves)
- 2–3 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder, for finishing
- Fine dark chocolate shavings (optional)

## Method

### 1) Make the coffee soak
Brew strong espresso and let it cool to room temperature (hot coffee over-softens the biscuits). Stir in alcohol and sugar if using. Pour into a shallow bowl.

### 2) Make the mascarpone cream

**Classic (raw egg):**
In a stand mixer or with a hand mixer, whisk **egg yolks** and **sugar** until thick and pale, 3–5 minutes. Add **mascarpone** and beat until smooth and fluffy. In a clean bowl, whip **egg whites** to stiff peaks, then gently fold into the mascarpone mixture in two additions.

**Sabayon (cooked egg, safer):**
Whisk yolks and sugar in a heatproof bowl set over a gently simmering bain-marie until thick, pale, and reaching 70–75°C (160°F). Remove from heat, cool slightly, then whisk in mascarpone until smooth. Fold in whipped cream instead of egg whites.

**No-egg / lighter:**
Whisk mascarpone with powdered sugar until smooth; fold in softly whipped heavy cream.

> Do not over-whip mascarpone — it can split. Fold gently to keep volume.

### 3) Assemble
Smear a thin layer of cream across the base of a **13×9-inch (33×23 cm)** dish (anchors the first layer). Quickly dip each ladyfinger in the coffee soak — **1–2 seconds per side**, center still slightly dry. Arrange in a snug single layer. Spread **half** the cream over top. Repeat with a second layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining cream. Smooth the top.

### 4) Chill
Cover and refrigerate for **at least 6 hours** (overnight is best) to set and meld flavors.

### 5) Serve
Sift cocoa powder generously over the top just before serving. Top with dark chocolate shavings if desired. Slice with a hot, dry knife.

## Notes & Tips
- **Make-ahead:** Best after 12–24 hr; up to 36 hr before serving. Keeps refrigerated up to 3 days.
- **Freezing:** Possible but softens texture; thaw overnight in the fridge.
- **Soggy layers:** Over-soaked biscuits — dip faster and use cooled coffee.
- **Runny cream:** Under-whipped or over-mixed — chill your bowl and fold gently.
- **Split cream:** Over-whipped mascarpone — fold in a little cold cream to rescue.

## Variations
- **Alcohol-free:** Coffee only; add a drop of vanilla.
- **Egg-free:** Use whipped cream instead of eggs.
- **Tea-misu:** Strong black tea or matcha (sweeten slightly) as the soak.
- **Chocolate-forward:** Ripple in 50–75 g melted dark chocolate into the cream.
- **Fruit accent:** Thin berries or macerated stone fruit between cream layers (sparingly).
- **Greek Yogurt, Lime & Ginger:** Replace half (or all) mascarpone with strained Greek yogurt (hang in cheesecloth 2–12 hr). Fold in lime zest (1–2 tsp) and fresh micro-planed ginger (½–1 tsp), plus honey/sugar to taste.
