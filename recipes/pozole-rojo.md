# Pozole Rojo
**Serves:** 8  
**Prep time:** 30 minutes

### For the soup:
- 3 lbs pork shoulder (or butt), cut into large chunks  
- 1 lb pork neck bones or trotters (optional, for richness)  
- 1 whole white onion, peeled and halved  
- 5 garlic cloves, peeled  
- 2 tsp salt  
- 10‚Äì12 cups water  
- 2 (25 oz) cans white hominy, drained and rinsed (or 2 cups dried hominy, soaked and cooked)

### For the red chile sauce:
- 5 dried guajillo chiles  
- 3 dried ancho chiles  
- 2 garlic cloves  
- ¬Ω tsp Mexican oregano  
- 1 cup pork broth (from soup pot)  

### Garnishes (optional but recommended):
- Finely shredded cabbage or lettuce  
- Thinly sliced radishes  
- Diced white onion  
- Dried Mexican oregano  
- Lime wedges  
- Crushed tostadas or tortilla chips  
- Avocado slices  
- Chile flakes or hot sauce (if desired)

---

## üî• Instructions

### 1. Simmer the pork:
In a large pot, add pork shoulder, bones (if using), onion, garlic, salt, and water. Bring to a boil, skim off foam, then reduce to a simmer. Cook for 1.5 to 2 hours until the pork is tender and shreds easily. Remove onion and garlic.

### 2. Make the red chile sauce:
While the pork simmers, remove stems and seeds from guajillo and ancho chiles. Toast lightly on a hot skillet (30 sec per side), then soak in hot water for 20 minutes. Blend soaked chiles with garlic, oregano, and 1 cup of broth until smooth. Strain through a sieve.

### 3. Add sauce and hominy:
Add the red chile sauce and drained hominy to the pot. Stir and simmer uncovered for 30‚Äì45 minutes to meld flavors. Taste and adjust salt.

### 4. Serve and garnish:
Ladle pozole into bowls. Garnish with cabbage, radish, onion, lime, and toppings of choice.

## üìù Notes
- **Dried hominy:** Soak overnight and simmer separately ~2 hours before adding.
- **Chicken version:** Use bone-in chicken thighs, reduce simmering time.
- **Make-ahead:** Even better the next day!
