# Prawns in Coconut Masala

**Source:** Elegant & Inspired Vij's Indian Cuisine Cookbook (via The Dirty Apron)

**Yield:** 6 servings

A quick, fragrant Indian prawn dish from Vij's cookbook — prawns cooked in a deeply caramelised onion and tomato masala with coconut milk, finished with green onions.

---

## Ingredients

- 30 prawns (shelled & deveined)
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp ghee (or canola oil)
- ½ tbsp cumin seeds
- 2 large onions, chopped
- 3 large ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp coconut milk (stirred)
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar (or balsamic)
- 2 tsp chopped green chilies
- 3 bunches green onions (white & green parts chopped)

---

## Method

1. **Prepare the prawns:** Place prawns in a colander and rinse under cold water. Allow excess water to drain. In a bowl, combine prawns and 1 tsp of salt. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside in the refrigerator while you are making the coconut masala.

2. **Make the masala:** In a large frying pan, melt the ghee on medium-high heat (or heat oil for 1 minute). Add cumin seeds and allow them to sizzle for 30 seconds. Add onions and sauté 5 to 8 minutes or until dark brown but not burned. Stir in tomatoes, coconut milk, vinegar, chilies, and the remaining 1 tsp of salt. Cook for 5 minutes, or until tomatoes are cooked through. Add green onions and stir well.

3. **Cook the prawns:** Add prawns, stirring constantly until they become pinkish-orange. This will take about 3 minutes. Immediately remove from the heat.

---

## To Serve

Place 5 prawns on each of six small shallow plates. Top each serving with one-sixth of the coconut masala. Alternatively, divide the coconut masala evenly among six small shallow plates, then top with 5 prawns per plate.

---

## Notes

- Can add Garam Masala for extra depth.
- Substitute fish for the prawns.
- Masala can be prepared in advance (without the coconut milk — add it when reheating).
- Can serve with rice or bread and bruschetta.
