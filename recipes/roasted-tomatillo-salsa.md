# Roasted Tomatillo Salsa

**Source:** [Rick Bayless](https://www.rickbayless.com/recipe/roasted-tomatillo-salsa-2/)

**Yield:** 1½ cups

Roasting adds welcome sweetness to naturally acidic tomatillos, with charred bits providing rustic flavor. The result balances sweetness and tartness with mellow roasted elements.

---

## Ingredients

- 12 ounces (6–8 medium) tomatillos, husked and rinsed
- 3–4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- Fresh hot green chiles (serrano or jalapeño), to taste
- ½ medium white onion
- 6–8 fresh cilantro sprigs
- Salt, to taste

---

## Instructions

1. **Roast:** Arrange tomatillos, unpeeled garlic, and chiles on a rimmed baking sheet. Broil 4 inches from heat for approximately 6 minutes until browned on one side. Flip and continue roasting until the other side is charred and the garlic feels soft.

2. **Blend:** Cool until manageable. Peel the garlic. Transfer all ingredients, including any accumulated juices, to a blender or food processor. Pulse to your desired consistency — a smooth texture with some remaining texture is preferred.

3. **Finish:** Bundle the cilantro and slice thinly. Dice the onion into ¼-inch pieces and rinse to mellow the raw flavour. Stir both into the salsa. Add water a tablespoon at a time if needed to reach the right consistency. Season with approximately 1 teaspoon salt.
