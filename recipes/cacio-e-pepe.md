# Cacio e Pepe

**Source:** Campagnolo Roma (Hastings Sunrise Cooks, p. 37) — by Joachim Hayward

**Yield:** 1–2 servings

A simple yet decadent Roman pasta dish of spaghetti, pecorino romano, black pepper, and butter. One of four staple Roman dishes on the menu at Campagnolo Roma since day one.

---

## Ingredients

- 6 oz (180 g) dried spaghetti noodles
- 5 tbsps unsalted butter, softened
- 2 tbsps pecorino romano cheese, grated
- 2 tbsps water
- A pinch of freshly cracked black pepper

---

## Instructions

1. **Boil the pasta:** Bring a pot of water to a boil and season with sea salt (it should have the same salinity as sea water). Boil dried pasta for 11–15 minutes or until al dente (firm to the bite). Drain into a colander and set aside.

2. **Make the sauce:** In a medium-sized fry pan, melt 4 tbsps of the butter with 2 tbsps of water. Add the cooked and drained noodles, then add the remaining 1 tbsp butter and half of the pecorino cheese. Toss until creamy.

3. **Serve:** Plate into a bowl and top with the remaining pecorino and freshly cracked pepper.

## Notes

- **Dairy-free:** Omit butter and substitute margarine or olive oil (pecorino romano is a sheep's milk cheese).
- **Gluten-free:** Substitute gluten-free pasta.
