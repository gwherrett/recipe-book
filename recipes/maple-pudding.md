# Maple Pudding

A traditional self-saucing pudding cake that bakes in a pool of maple syrup, creating a sweet, caramelized dessert with a tender cake top and syrupy bottom.

---

## Ingredients

### For the Batter:
- 1/4 cup shortening
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1 cup flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- Pinch salt

### For Baking:
- Maple syrup (enough for 1 inch depth in bottom of casserole)

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the casserole:**
   Pour maple syrup into the bottom of a casserole dish to a depth of about 1 inch.

2. **Make the batter:**
   Cream together shortening and sugar. Add milk and mix well. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, and salt. Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients and mix until just combined.

3. **Assemble and bake:**
   Drop spoonfuls of batter over the maple syrup in the casserole (do not stir). Bake at 350Â°F for 40 minutes, until the top is golden and set.

4. **Serve:**
   Serve warm, spooning the maple syrup sauce from the bottom over each portion. Excellent with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.

---

## Notes

- The batter will rise and cook on top while the maple syrup creates a sauce underneath
- Use pure maple syrup for best flavor
- Best served warm from the oven
- Traditional comfort food dessert, likely Canadian or New England origin
