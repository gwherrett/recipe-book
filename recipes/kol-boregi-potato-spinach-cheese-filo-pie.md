# Kol Böreği (Potato, Spinach & Cheese Filo Pie)

**Yield:** 6–8 servings

A Cypriot-Turkish spiral börek filled with grated potato, wilted spinach, and a blend of white and grated cheeses, wrapped in layers of buttered filo and baked until deeply golden and crunchy.

---

## Ingredients

- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large or 2 small onions, very finely chopped
- 1 lb 2 oz (500 g) Cyprus or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled, coarsely grated, squeezed, and drained in a sieve to get rid of excess starchy juices
- 2 tbsp Turkish sweet red pepper paste (*tatlı biber salçası*)
- 2 large garlic cloves, crushed
- 14 oz (400 g) baby spinach leaves, washed and roughly chopped
- 7 oz (200 g) Cypriot ricotta (*nor peyniri* or *anari*), *beyaz peynir* (Turkish white cheese) or feta
- 3½ oz (100 g) *kaşar peyniri* or Gouda cheese, coarsely grated
- 4 tbsp finely chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
- 1 tsp *pul biber*
- Grated zest of 1 lemon
- 1 tsp dried mint
- ¾ tsp salt
- ¾ tsp black pepper
- ¾ cup (175 g) unsalted butter
- 12 large sheets of filo pastry
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds

---

## Instructions

1. **Preheat oven:** Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).

2. **Cook the onion and potato:** Heat the oil in a large nonstick pot over medium heat and soften the onion for 8–10 minutes until completely translucent and slightly caramelized. Add the potatoes and cook for a further 5–6 minutes, stirring continuously until they soften — if some of the potato edges crisp up a little, even better.

3. **Add pepper paste, garlic, and spinach:** Add the sweet red pepper paste and garlic, stir well, then add the spinach. Cook the spinach until the leaves have fully wilted. Transfer to a large dish to cool.

4. **Mix the cheese filling:** In a separate bowl, crumble in the white cheese, then stir in the grated cheese, parsley, *pul biber*, lemon zest, and dried mint.

5. **Combine and portion:** Once the spinach and potato mixture has cooled, gently stir in the cheese mixture. Season to taste with the salt and pepper. If using feta, you might need to use slightly less salt as it can be salty depending on the brand. Divide the mixture into six equal portions.

6. **Prepare the baking sheet:** Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

7. **Clarify the butter:** In a small saucepan, melt the butter. Once melted, carefully skim off the froth that will have formed and discard.

8. **Assemble the spiral:** Lay one sheet of filo horizontally on a clean surface, brush it all over with the melted butter, lay another sheet of filo on top, brush with a little more butter, then spread one portion of the filling mixture all along the bottom edge of the filo nearest to you. Roll the pastry away from the edge nearest to you into a log shape, then slowly coil it around into a spiral (like a snail shell), roughly 4 inches (10 cm) in diameter. Use the warmth of your hands to shape and cup the spiral to prevent it from splitting. Place it in the center of the lined baking sheet, then repeat the method with the remaining five portions of filo and filling, but instead of rolling into spirals, lift the long filo roll straight on to the baking sheet, connecting it to the end of the previous roll and coiling it around until you have one huge spiral.

9. **Glaze and bake:** Brush the spiral with the remaining melted butter first, then liberally, all over, with the beaten egg, not forgetting the sides too (the egg will give the pastry its beautiful golden color and crunchy exterior). Sprinkle with the sesame seeds and bake in the oven, on the bottom shelf, for 40–50 minutes until beautifully golden brown all over.

---

## Notes

- Use the best quality filo you can afford and find. Look for packs with large sheets, often marketed in Turkish grocery stores and international supermarkets as *baklavalık yufka* (baklava filo). Cheaper filo that dries up too quickly and flakes too much when shaping, or sheets that are far too small, will be disappointing.
