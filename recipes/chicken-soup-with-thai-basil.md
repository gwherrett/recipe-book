# Chicken Soup with Thai Basil

## Ingredients
- 500g chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 liter chicken broth
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup Thai basil leaves
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Ingredients
- Wash the chicken and cut it into bite-sized pieces.
- Peel and slice the carrots, chop the onion, and mince the garlic.

### 2. Cook the Chicken
- Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
- Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are slightly browned on all sides. Remove the chicken and set aside.

### 3. Sauté Vegetables
- In the same pot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent.
- Add the sliced carrots and cook for another 2–3 minutes.

### 4. Simmer the Soup
- Return the chicken to the pot.
- Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil.
- Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.

### 5. Season the Soup
- Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, lime juice, and sugar.
- Add salt and pepper to taste.
- Let the soup simmer for another 5 minutes.

### 6. Add Thai Basil
- Just before serving, stir in the Thai basil leaves.
- Let them wilt slightly in the hot soup.

### 7. Serve
- Ladle the soup into bowls.
- Serve hot, ideally with a side of steamed rice or noodles if desired.
