# Spicy Curry Ketchup

**Category:** Condiments & Sauces  
**Tags:** Spicy, Curry, Ketchup, Heinz-based  

**Summary:**  
A hot and flavorful curry ketchup made with Heinz as the base, layered with warm Indian spices and chili heat. This sauce is perfect for dipping fries, slathering on burgers, or drizzling over sausages. Quick to make, with bold flavor that develops further overnight.

---

## 🧂 Ingredients

- 1 cup Heinz ketchup  
- 1 tbsp curry powder (madras for extra heat)  
- 1 tsp smoked paprika  
- ½ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)  
- ½ tsp ground cumin  
- ½ tsp ground coriander  
- ½ tsp garlic powder  
- ½ tsp onion powder  
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar  
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce  
- 1 tbsp brown sugar or honey  
- ½ tsp salt  
- ½ tsp black pepper  
- ½ tsp chili flakes (optional, for extra heat)  
- 1–2 tbsp water (to adjust consistency)  

---

## 🍳 Method

1. In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine Heinz ketchup, curry powder, smoked paprika, cayenne, cumin, coriander, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper.  
2. Stir in the apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, and brown sugar or honey.  
3. Let the mixture simmer gently for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the spices are bloomed and the flavors deepen.  
4. Add 1–2 tablespoons of water to thin it out to your preferred consistency, if needed.  
5. Taste and adjust seasoning—more cayenne or chili flakes will boost the heat.  
6. Let cool before transferring to a clean jar or squeeze bottle.

---

## 💡 Notes

- Keeps refrigerated for up to 2 weeks.  
- For a smoother texture, blend the sauce before cooling.  
- Optional add-in: a splash of hot sauce or a pinch of allspice for a deeper kick.

---

## 🍴 Serving Suggestions

- As a dip for fries, roasted potatoes, or onion rings  
- Alongside bratwurst or currywurst  
- As a burger condiment  
- Stirred into mayo for a spicy curry aioli
