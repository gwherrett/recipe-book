# Chili Powder (Ancho-Based)

A rich, flavorful chili powder made with dried chilies other than cayenne. This version uses ancho and other optional chilies for a deep, complex taste suitable for soups, stews, tacos, and more.

## 🌶️ Ingredients

- 3–4 dried **Ancho chilies** (mild, smoky)
- 2–3 dried **Guajillo chilies** (optional, mild-medium, sweet/tangy)
- 1–2 dried **Chipotle chilies** (optional, smoky heat)
- 1–2 dried **Árbol chilies** (optional, hot – for more heat)
- **Optional additional spices:**
  - 1 tsp garlic powder
  - 1 tsp onion powder
  - 1 tsp ground cumin
  - 1 tsp dried oregano
  - 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  - Salt to taste

## 🔥 Instructions

1. **Prepare the Chilies:**
   - Remove stems and seeds from dried chilies.

2. **Toast:**
   - In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the chilies for 1–2 minutes per side until fragrant (do not burn).

3. **Grind:**
   - Break toasted chilies into small pieces.
   - Use a spice grinder, coffee grinder, or mortar and pestle to grind into a fine powder.

4. **Mix:**
   - Combine ground chilies with any optional spices. Mix thoroughly.

5. **Store:**
   - Store in an airtight jar in a cool, dry place. Keeps for several months.

## 📝 Notes

- Ancho chilies are dried poblano peppers and are very mild but flavorful.
- Adjust the type and ratio of chilies based on desired heat and flavor.
- Use about 1 tbsp in recipes calling for standard chili powder.
