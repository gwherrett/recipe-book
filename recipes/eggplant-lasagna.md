# Eggplant Lasagna

**Prep Time:** 45 minutes
**Cook Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
**Yield:** 8 servings

A classic Italian baked pasta dish layering tender roasted eggplant, rich tomato meat sauce, silky béchamel, and melted cheese between sheets of pasta.

---

## Ingredients

### For the eggplant

- 2 large eggplants (about 1 kg total), sliced ¼ inch thick
- 2 tsp salt
- 3 tbsp olive oil

### For the meat sauce

- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 500 g ground beef
- 2 cans (400 g each) crushed tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp sugar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Small handful fresh basil leaves

### For the béchamel

- 60 g unsalted butter
- 60 g plain flour
- 700 ml whole milk, warmed
- ¼ tsp freshly grated nutmeg
- Salt and white pepper to taste

### For assembly

- 12 dried lasagna sheets, cooked al dente and drained
- 200 g fresh mozzarella, torn
- 80 g Parmesan, finely grated

---

## Instructions

1. **Salt the eggplant:** Spread eggplant slices on a rack set over a baking sheet and sprinkle both sides with salt. Let stand 30 minutes, then pat dry with paper towels.

2. **Roast the eggplant:** Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Brush eggplant slices with olive oil and arrange in a single layer on baking sheets. Roast for 20–25 minutes, flipping once halfway, until golden and tender. Set aside.

3. **Make the meat sauce:** Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Cook onion until softened, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it up, until browned. Stir in tomato paste and cook 2 minutes. Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, and sugar. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer uncovered for 25–30 minutes until thickened. Stir in fresh basil and remove from heat.

4. **Make the béchamel:** Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add flour and cook, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes to form a paste. Gradually whisk in warm milk a little at a time, ensuring each addition is smooth before adding more. Cook, stirring, until the sauce thickens enough to coat a spoon, about 8 minutes. Season with nutmeg, salt, and white pepper.

5. **Reduce oven temperature:** Lower oven to 180°C (350°F). Lightly grease a 33 × 23 cm (9 × 13 in) baking dish.

6. **Assemble:** Spread a thin layer of meat sauce over the base of the dish. Add a layer of lasagna sheets, trimming to fit. Spread over one-third of the remaining meat sauce, then one-third of the béchamel. Layer half the eggplant slices and scatter over one-third of the mozzarella and Parmesan. Repeat with another layer of pasta, meat sauce, béchamel, the remaining eggplant, and more cheese. Finish with a final layer of pasta sheets, the remaining béchamel spread to the edges, and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan on top.

7. **Bake:** Cover loosely with foil and bake for 35 minutes. Uncover and bake a further 15–20 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling.

8. **Rest and serve:** Remove from oven and let rest 15 minutes before cutting and serving.

---

## Notes

- Salting and patting the eggplant dry removes excess moisture so the layers don't turn watery.
- The lasagna can be assembled up to 24 hours ahead, covered, and refrigerated — add 10–15 minutes to the covered baking time.
- Leftovers keep refrigerated for up to 4 days and reheat well in a 180°C oven.
