# Classic Gremolata

**Yield:** 4 servings (as garnish)

A bright, fragrant Italian condiment of parsley, lemon zest, and garlic. Traditionally served over osso buco, it lifts grilled fish, steak, roasted vegetables, and pasta with sharp, fresh flavour.

---

## Ingredients

- ¼ cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated or minced
- 1 lemon, zested
- Pinch of flaky salt

---

## Method

1. **Zest the lemon:** Use a microplane — yellow outer layer only, avoid the bitter white pith.

2. **Chop the parsley:** Finely chop by hand with a sharp knife (no food processor — it bruises the herb).

3. **Integrate:** Add lemon zest and garlic on top of the parsley on the board, then chop together briefly to combine.

4. **Season:** Transfer to a bowl, add salt, stir, and taste.

5. **Serve:** Use immediately as a garnish — best the same day.

---

## Variations

- **Citrus** — swap lemon for orange or lime zest
- **Herb** — use mint, cilantro, basil, or tarragon instead of (or with) parsley
- **Sauce** — add 1–2 tbsp olive oil; keeps up to a week refrigerated
- **Crunch** — fold in toasted pine nuts or pistachios
- **Brine** — add minced capers or finely grated Parmesan
- **Mellow** — replace garlic with finely minced shallot

## Notes

- Traditionally served over osso buco; great on grilled fish, steak, roasted veg, pasta
- Can be made up to 6 hours ahead — cover and refrigerate
