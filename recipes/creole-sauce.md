# Creole Sauce (with White Pepper)

A classic Louisiana-style Creole sauce, full of bold tomato flavor, herbs, and just the right amount of heat. Great for shrimp, chicken, fish, or spooned over rice.

## üçÖ Ingredients
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil or butter  
- 1 small onion, finely chopped  
- 1/2 green bell pepper, finely chopped  
- 1 celery stalk, finely chopped  
- 3 cloves garlic, minced  
- 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes (or 2 fresh tomatoes, chopped)  
- 2 tbsp tomato paste  
- 1/2 cup chicken or seafood stock (or water)  
- 1 tsp paprika  
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)  
- 1/2 tsp dried thyme  
- 1/2 tsp dried oregano  
- 1/4 tsp white pepper  
- 1 bay leaf  
- 1 tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)  
- Salt and black pepper, to taste  
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce  
- Hot sauce (optional, to taste)  
- 1 tbsp chopped parsley (for garnish)  
- Green onion (optional, for garnish)

2. **Add garlic:**  
   Stir in garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. **Add tomatoes & seasonings:**  
   Add diced tomatoes (with juices), tomato paste, and stock. Stir to combine.

4. **Spice it up:**  
   Add paprika, cayenne, thyme, oregano, white pepper, bay leaf, sugar (if using), Worcestershire, salt, and pepper. Add hot sauce if desired.

5. **Simmer:**  
   Reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 20‚Äì30 minutes until thickened, stirring occasionally.

6. **Finish & serve:**  
   Remove bay leaf. Taste and adjust seasoning. Garnish with chopped parsley and green onion if using.
