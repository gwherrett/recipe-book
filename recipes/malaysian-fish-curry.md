# Malaysian Fish Curry

**Source:** [Season with Spice](http://blog.seasonwithspice.com/2011/08/malaysian-fish-curry-by-nazlina-hussin.html) — contributed by Nazlina Hussin of Pickles and Spices

**Yield:** 2–3 servings

A traditional Malay-style fish curry from Penang, built on a fragrant base of pounded shallots and garlic, fenugreek spice mixture, and curry leaves. Tamarind juice adds sourish-sweetness, and coconut milk finishes the sauce with body and richness.

---

## Ingredients

- 500g black cod (or fish steaks)
- 37g fish curry powder, mixed with 200ml water
- 150ml coconut milk
- 50g shallots (or onion), peeled and cut lengthwise
- ½ head of garlic
- ½ inch ginger, cut into matchsticks
- ½ torch ginger flower (bunga kantan), chopped finely
- ½ pressed cup Vietnamese mint leaves (or normal mint)
- ½ stalk lemongrass, chopped finely
- 3 stalks of curry leaves
- 1 Tbsp tamarind paste, soaked in 200ml water, then strained — pulp and seeds removed
- 1½ big tomatoes, cut into wedges
- 3 okra, top removed and a slit made halfway
- 2 green chilis, cut lengthwise in quarters
- 75ml cooking oil
- Salt and sugar to taste

### Spice mixture

- ½ tsp fenugreek mixture (halba campur) — equal parts fenugreek, cumin, fennel, mustard seeds, and urad dhal

---

## Method

1. **Pound aromatics:** Pound shallots and garlic together until fine.

2. **Fry spices:** Heat oil until slightly smoky. Add the fenugreek spice mixture and let it sizzle for a few seconds.

3. **Build the base:** Add the pounded shallots and garlic, then the curry leaves and ginger.

4. **Cook the paste:** When fragrant, add in the curry paste. Simmer on low heat until the oil floats to the top and turns a velvety red.

5. **Add fish and vegetables:** Add the fish and all vegetables, together with the tamarind juice, torch ginger, lemongrass, and Vietnamese mint. Simmer slowly.

6. **Turn fish, add coconut milk:** Once one side of the fish is cooked, turn it over and add in the coconut milk.

7. **Finish:** Simmer another five minutes or so to let everything mingle. Add more mint if you wish.

8. **Season:** Season with enough salt and sugar to taste.
