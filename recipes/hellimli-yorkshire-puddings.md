# Hellimli Yorkshire Puddings

TRADITIONAL RECIPE, CHEATER'S VERSION

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes (+ at least 1 hour chilling time in fridge)
**Cook Time:** 50 minutes
**Yield:** 12

A classic British Yorkshire pudding — light, crispy, and deeply risen — with an optional Cypriot twist of grated hellim (halloumi), dried mint, and seeds baked into the centre. Includes a gluten-free variation.

---

## Ingredients

### Regular Yorkshire Pudding Mix

- Scant 1 cup (200 ml) milk (whole, 2% or dairy-free such as almond, soy or oat are fine)
- Scant 1 cup (100 g) all-purpose flour
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 4 medium eggs
- 3 tbsp sunflower/vegetable/rice bran oil

### For the Gluten-Free Yorkshire Pudding Mix

- Scant 1 cup (200 ml) milk (whole, 2% or dairy-free such as almond, soy or oat are fine)
- 4 large eggs
- 1¼ oz (50 g) gluten-free all-purpose flour
- 1¾ cups (150 g) cornstarch
- ½ tsp fine sea salt
- 3 tbsp sunflower/vegetable/rice bran oil

### For the Hellimli Topping (Optional)

- 3½ oz (100 g) hellim (halloumi cheese), coarsely grated
- 1 tbsp dried mint
- 1 tsp sesame seeds
- 1 tsp nigella seeds

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the batter:** In a large measuring jug, whisk together the milk, eggs and all-purpose flour (plus the cornstarch if you are making the gluten-free version). Whisk until completely smooth and no lumps are visible, stir through the salt, then cover the jug and put it in the fridge for at least an hour or overnight.

2. **Preheat the oven:** Preheat the oven to 480°F (250°C).

3. **Oil the muffin pan:** When the oven is hot, fill each of the 12 holes of a deep, nonstick muffin pan with around ¼ teaspoon of the oil, ensuring you also grease the sides, to easily pop out the puddings once they're done.

4. **Heat the oil:** Place the muffin pan on the top rack of the oven for 15–20 minutes until the oil is smoking.

5. **Prepare the hellimli topping:** If you are making the hellimli Yorkshire puddings, then mix together the topping ingredients while the muffin pan is in the oven. If you are making plain ones, leave out this step.

6. **Stir the batter:** Take the Yorkshire pudding batter out of the fridge and give it a quick but thorough stir, as the flour has a tendency to settle at the bottom of the jug while it rests in the fridge (especially with the gluten-free batter).

7. **Fill and bake:** Carefully take the muffin pan out of the oven and place it on an even, heatproof surface. In one swift motion, quickly pour equal measures of the batter around ¾ of the way up into the center of the sizzling hot oil in each of the 12 holes of the muffin pan without topping them up. If making the hellimli Yorkshire puddings, add a heaped teaspoon of the hellimli mixture to the center of the batter in each hole, then quickly and carefully place the muffin pan back on the middle rack of the oven, and immediately close the oven door. Turn the oven down to 410°F (210°C) and bake for 25–30 minutes. Do not open the oven door at all until the Yorkshire puddings are cooked and do not be tempted to take them out too early, otherwise they'll just collapse.

8. **Serve or freeze:** Once cooked, they should all be a rich, dark golden-brown color, look deliciously crispy and have fully risen. Take the Yorkshire puddings straight out of the muffin pan and onto a cooling rack, and either serve immediately, or allow to cool completely, put them in freezer bags and freeze for up to 6 months.

---

## Note

To reheat (from frozen, as there is no need to defrost), place in a preheated oven at 410°F (210°C) for 8–10 minutes, and they'll taste as if you've just made them.
