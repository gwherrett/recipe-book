# Momofuku Bo Ssäm

A legendary Korean-American pork feast by David Chang, adapted for home kitchens.

---

## 🥩 Ingredients (serves 6–8)

### Pork
- 1 whole bone‑in Boston pork butt (8–10 lb / ~3.5–4.5 kg)  
- 1 cup granulated sugar  
- 1 cup + 1 Tbsp kosher salt  
- 7 Tbsp light brown sugar  

### Ginger‑Scallion Sauce (makes ~3 cups)
- 2½ cups thinly sliced scallions (white + green)  
- ½ cup minced fresh ginger  
- ¼ cup neutral oil (e.g., grapeseed)  
- 1½ tsp light soy sauce  
- ¾ tsp sherry vinegar  
- ¾ tsp kosher salt (plus extra to taste)  

### Ssäm Sauce (~1 cup)
- 1 Tbsp ssämjang (fermented bean‑chile paste)  
- ½ Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)  
- ¼ cup sherry vinegar  
- ¼ cup neutral oil  

### Accompaniments
- 1 dozen shucked oysters  
- 1 cup napa cabbage kimchi + optional 1 cup kimchi purée  
- 2 cups cooked short‑grain rice  
- 3–4 heads Bibb or lettuce leaves  
- Coarse sea salt (e.g., Maldon)  

---

## 🔪 Method

### 1. Cure the pork
- Place the pork in a snug roasting pan.  
- Combine 1 cup sugar + 1 cup salt; rub thoroughly over the pork. Discard excess.  
- Wrap and refrigerate at least 6 hours or overnight.

### 2. Roast low and slow
- Preheat oven to 300 °F (150 °C).  
- Discard any released juices, roast pork uncovered, basting hourly with juices/fat for ~6 hours, until a knife moves through easily and meat pulls apart.

### 3. Make sauces (can be prepped while pork cooks)
- **Ginger‑Scallion Sauce**: whisk all listed ingredients; taste and stash in fridge for 15 min minimum.  
- **Ssäm Sauce**: mix until uniform; refrigerates for weeks.

### 4. Glaze and crisp
- When pork is tender, preheat oven to 500 °F (260 °C).  
- Mix remaining 1 Tbsp salt + 7 Tbsp brown sugar; rub all over pork.  
- Roast 10–15 minutes until the sugar forms a crackly, caramelized crust.

### 5. Serve bo ssäm–style
- Slice or pull pork and let rest slightly.  
- Lay out lettuce leaves; top with rice, pork, a raw oyster, kimchi, sauces.  
- Season with coarse salt; wrap and enjoy.

---

## 👩‍🍳 Pro Tips

- **Make it yours**: Add roasted garlic or chili-crunch glaze for an extra kick.  
- **Cook time varies**: Some cooks report longer roast times (~12 hours) for pull-apart tenderness.  
- **More crispy skin?** Try a trotter + hock combo for extra crackle and gelatin.

| Task | When |
|------|------|

| Prep oysters, rice, lettuce, kimchi | Just before serving |
