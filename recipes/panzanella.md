# Panzanella (Tuscan Bread Salad)

**Category:** Salads & Bowls\
**Tags:** Italian, vegetarian, summer

**Summary:**\
A classic Tuscan salad made with ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, red onion,
and toasted bread cubes tossed in a tangy olive oil and vinegar
dressing. Perfect for using up stale bread and celebrating peak summer
produce.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Ingredients:

-   4 cups stale crusty bread, cut into cubes\
-   3 large tomatoes, cut into chunks\
-   1 cucumber, sliced\
-   1 red onion, thinly sliced\
-   1 bunch of fresh basil, torn\
-   1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil\
-   3 tbsp red wine vinegar\
-   Salt and pepper, to taste\
-   *(Optional)* 1/2 cup mozzarella or feta cheese, cubed

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Method:

1.  **Prep the Bread:** If the bread is not stale, toast cubes in the
    oven at 375°F (190°C) for \~10 minutes until crisp but not hard.\
2.  **Combine Vegetables:** In a large bowl, mix tomatoes, cucumber, red
    onion, and basil.\
3.  **Make Dressing:** Whisk olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and
    pepper until emulsified.\
4.  **Assemble Salad:** Add bread to vegetables, pour over dressing, and
    toss until evenly coated. Add cheese if using.\
5.  **Rest & Serve:** Let sit for at least 30 minutes for the bread to
    absorb flavors. Adjust seasoning before serving.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Notes:**\
- Add capers or olives for a briny twist.\
- For a heartier version, include grilled chicken or roasted peppers.\
- Best enjoyed at room temperature within a few hours of mixing.
