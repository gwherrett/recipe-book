# Malay Curry Beef Brisket

**Source:** Hastings Sunrise Cooks (Ipoh Asian House, by Mr. Hu)

**Yield:** 4 servings

Curry beef originated from India, but soon became a popular dish in different areas of Southeastern Asia as well. As the recipes evolved, Malaysian curry differed from traditional Indian curry through the use of coconut milk and coconut powder. Mr. Hu continued to enhance this traditional recipe using various spices in order to create a unique dish.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 lb beef brisket, cut into 1 inch squares
- 1½ tbsps lemongrass
- ½ cup onion, chopped
- 2 shallots
- 1 tsp garlic
- ½ cup coconut milk
- ¼ cup coconut powder
- ¼ cup Malaysian meat curry powder
- 1 tsp curry leaves
- 1 tbsp oil
- 2 cups water
- ½ tsp salt
- ½ tsp sugar

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the beef:** Wash and cut the beef brisket. Bring a pot of water to boil, then add the brisket. Once the beef is added to the water, turn off the heat and let it sit in the water for 1 hour.

2. **Make the spice blend:** Blend together lemongrass, onions, shallots, garlic, and curry leaves in a food processor.

3. **Fry the blend:** Place the blend in a wok and stir-fry over medium heat until golden brown. Add the curry powder and oil to the mixture in the wok.

4. **Add the beef:** Remove the beef from the hot water and blanch under cold water. Add the beef to the mixture in the wok.

5. **Simmer:** Add coconut milk, coconut powder, sugar, salt, and water, then mix. Turn the heat down to a simmer and stir occasionally. Cook for 25 minutes, and then serve.
