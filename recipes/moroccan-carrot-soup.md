# Moroccan Carrot Soup

**Category:** Soups  
**Cuisine:** Moroccan-Inspired  
**Tags:** Healthy, High-Fiber, Low-Calorie, Anti-Inflammatory

---

## **Why This Soup Is Healthy**
- **High in fiber** → supports digestion and stabilizes blood sugar.  
- **Low calorie** (approx. 155 kcal/serving).  
- **Rich in antioxidants** from carrots and cumin.  
- **Probiotics** from yogurt (optional).  
- **Anti-inflammatory spices** → cumin, allspice.  
- **Low sugar**, naturally sweetened by carrots.

---

## **Ingredients**
- 2 Tbsp butter *(or olive oil for a lighter option)*
- 1 cup white onion, chopped
- 1 lb carrots, peeled and diced (about 2⅔ cups)
- 2½ cups low-salt chicken broth *(or vegetable broth)*
- 1½ tsp cumin seeds
- 1 Tbsp honey *(reduce to 1 tsp for low sugar)*
- 1 tsp fresh lemon juice
- ⅛ tsp ground allspice
- ½ cup plain yogurt, stirred *(Greek yogurt optional)*
- Salt and pepper, to taste

---

## **Instructions**

### 1. Cook the base
Heat butter (or olive oil) in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add onion and sauté ~2 minutes. Add carrots and stir.

### 2. Simmer
Add broth, bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, and simmer until carrots are **very tender**, about 20 minutes.

### 3. Toast the cumin
Toast cumin seeds in a dry pan until fragrant (4–5 minutes). Cool, then grind.

### 4. Purée
Remove soup from heat and blend until smooth. Return to same pot.

### 5. Season
Whisk in honey, lemon juice, and allspice. Season with salt and pepper.

### 6. Serve
Ladle into bowls. Drizzle yogurt on top and sprinkle generously with toasted cumin.

---

## **Optional Health Swaps**
- Replace butter with **olive oil** → lower saturated fat.
- Use **low-fat or Greek yogurt** for higher protein.
- Add **¾ cup red lentils** during simmering → boosts protein and fiber.
- Omit honey for a **naturally sweet**, no-added-sugar version.
- Make fully vegetarian with **vegetable broth**.
