# Lo Bak Go (White Radish Cake)

**Category:** Dim Sum / Savory Cakes  
**Tags:** Chinese, steamed, radish, rice flour, dim sum, Lunar New Year  

---

## Summary

A traditional Cantonese dish made from shredded white radish (lo bak), rice flour, and savory stir-fried ingredients like Chinese sausage, dried shrimp, and mushrooms. The mixture is steamed into a soft, sliceable cake that can be served as-is or pan-fried for a crispy finish. A staple at dim sum tables and often enjoyed during Lunar New Year.

---

## Ingredients

### Base
- 4–5 lbs white radish (lo bak), peeled and shredded
- 400 g rice flour
- 4 tbsp cornstarch
- 2½ cups water (for cooking radish)
- 2 cups water (for mixing with flour)

### Filling
- Small handful dried shrimp, soaked and drained
- 1–2 Chinese sausages (lap cheong), finely chopped
- 3–4 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and diced
- 1–2 tsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp cooking wine
- ¼ tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp chicken broth mix (powdered)

### Other
- Oil for greasing the pan

---

## Method

1. **Cook the Radish:**
   - In a pot, add shredded white radish and 2½ cups of water.
   - Cook over medium heat until radish softens. Turn off heat and set aside.

2. **Prepare Filling:**
   - Stir-fry soaked dried shrimp, chopped sausage, and diced mushrooms.
   - Season with soy sauce, cooking wine, white pepper, and chicken broth mix.

3. **Make Batter:**
   - In a large bowl, combine 2 cups water, rice flour, and cornstarch. Mix until smooth.

4. **Combine:**
   - Add the batter to the cooked radish and mix well.
   - Stir in the stir-fried ingredients and combine thoroughly.

5. **Steam:**
   - Grease a cake pan or heatproof dish.
   - Pour in the mixture and level the surface.
   - Steam over high heat for 30–40 minutes, until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

6. **Cool and Serve:**
   - Let cool completely before slicing.
   - Optional: pan-fry slices until golden for extra flavor and texture.

---

## Notes

- Make ahead and refrigerate for 3–4 days.
- For extra umami, add a spoon of XO sauce to the filling.
- Can be frozen after steaming; reheat by pan-frying or re-steaming.
