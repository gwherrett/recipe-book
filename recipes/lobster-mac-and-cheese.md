# Lobster Mac n Cheese  
*By Rob Feenie*

**Yield:** Serves 6

## Béchamel Sauce
- **6 cups** milk
- **3 cloves** garlic, peeled and crushed
- **6 tbsp** unsalted butter
- **6 tbsp** all-purpose flour
- Pinch of freshly grated nutmeg
- Salt and freshly ground white pepper, to taste

### Method  
1. Combine milk and garlic in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, remove from heat, cover, and infuse for 10 minutes.  
2. Strain and discard solids.  
3. Make roux: melt butter, whisk in flour, cook 1 minute until light brown.  
4. Remove from heat briefly, then whisk in hot milk.  
5. Return to heat and bring to a boil, whisking until thickened.  
6. Add nutmeg, salt, pepper; simmer 3 minutes.

## Macaroni and Cheese
- **1½ lb** live lobster
- **1 tbsp** vegetable oil
- **12 oz** Irish or double‑smoked bacon, finely diced
- **6** shallots, finely chopped
- **6 tbsp** grated Emmental
- **6 tbsp** crumbled blue cheese
- **6 tbsp** grated sharp white cheddar
- **¾ cup** grated pecorino Romano
- **18 oz** dried succhietto pasta, cooked al dente

### Method  
1. Boil salted water; cook lobster 3 minutes. Cool, remove meat, dice.  
2. Preheat oven to **375°F**.  
3. Sauté bacon and shallots in oil for ~5 minutes.  
4. Reheat béchamel and whisk in Emmental, blue cheese, cheddar, and half the pecorino.  
5. Add half the bacon‑shallot mix and fold in lobster.  
6. Divide pasta into four 3‑cup casseroles; pour sauce over, covering pasta by ½ inch.  
7. Top with remaining pecorino and bacon‑shallot mix.  
8. Bake 10–15 minutes until bubbling.  
9. Broil 3–5 minutes until golden.
