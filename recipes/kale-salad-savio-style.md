# Kale Salad, Savio-Style

**Source:** [Scout Magazine](https://scoutmagazine.ca/we-tried-to-make-savio-volpes-awesome-kale-salad-at-home-with-delicious-results/)

**Yield:** 4–6 servings

A finely chiffonaded Tuscan kale salad inspired by Osteria Savio Volpe, dressed with lemon, olive oil, pecorino romano, and garlicky toasted panko breadcrumbs.

---

## Ingredients

- 2 large bunches Tuscan kale
- 1 large lemon (at least)
- Good olive oil (~3 tbsp total)
- ¼ cup panko bread crumbs
- 1 clove garlic, finely grated or minced
- ⅓ cup finely grated pecorino romano cheese (or sub equal amount of fresh parmesan)
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Handful of toasted pine nuts (optional)

---

## Directions

1. **Prep the kale:** Gently rinse the kale leaves and pat completely dry. Use a small sharp knife to cut out the thick rib from each leaf (the part that comprises the entire stem) and discard. Cut the leaves lengthwise into ½-inch sections — you should be left with lots of long, skinny strips of kale. If your kale leaves were unreasonably long (more than a foot or so), cut your strips in half to shorten them. Stack up several strips at a time, roll them up as neatly as you can, then cut very thin ribbons of kale, starting at one of the open ends of the roll. Repeat until all of the strips have been stacked, rolled, and cut. (This technique is called chiffonade — look it up online for visual support if needed.) Once done, you should have ~5 generous cups of finely shredded kale.

2. **Massage the kale:** Place the kale into a medium bowl and, using your hands, gently massage 2 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice into the ribbons. Let the kale sit while you prepare the remaining ingredients.

3. **Toast the breadcrumbs:** Heat a small pan over medium heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil, the panko crumbs, and garlic to the hot pan and stir quickly to combine. Keep stirring and tossing until the panko has become golden and the garlic is sizzling — this happens fairly quickly and burns fairly quickly, so stay vigilant. Set onto a small plate to cool.

4. **Dress and assemble:** Toss the kale ribbons with 1 tbsp olive oil, a good pinch of sea salt, and a generous grind of black pepper. Toss in the pecorino and pine nuts (if using). Stir in the cooled breadcrumbs, then taste and adjust. Add another splash of olive oil if you like your salads well-dressed, or another squeeze of lemon if you like them bright. Serve immediately.
