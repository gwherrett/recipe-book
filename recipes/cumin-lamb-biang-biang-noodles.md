# Spicy Cumin Lamb Biang Biang Noodles

**Source:** [The Woks of Life](https://thewoksoflife.com/cumin-lamb-biang-biang-noodles/)

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 1–2 hours dough resting)
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Yield:** 2 servings

Hand-pulled biang biang noodles from Shaanxi Province meet Xinjiang-style cumin lamb in this boldly spiced stir-fry. The wide, chewy noodles cling beautifully to the fragrant cumin-chili sauce.

---

## Ingredients

### For the Noodles

- 1½ cups bread flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ½ cup + 1 tablespoon water

### For the Lamb Marinade

- 8 oz. fatty lamb shoulder or chuck, thinly sliced
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch

### For the Spice Mixture

- 1½ tablespoons cumin seeds
- 2 teaspoons coriander seeds
- ¼ teaspoon freshly ground white pepper

### For the Sauce & Stir-Fry

- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon grated ginger
- 2 scallions, chopped (white and green parts separated)
- 7 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 long hot red or green chili pepper, de-seeded and thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons Shaoxing rice wine
- 3 tablespoons chili oil
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon black vinegar or rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
- ½ cup finely diced Chinese celery or regular celery (optional)
- 3 cabbage leaves, roughly chopped
- ½ cup garlic chives, roughly chopped (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the dough:** In the bowl of a stand mixer, combine the bread flour, salt, and water. Mix by hand until a scraggly dough forms. Attach the dough hook and knead on low speed for 20 minutes, until the dough is smooth, elastic, and doesn't stick to the sides of the bowl. Cover with plastic wrap or an overturned bowl and rest for 1 to 2 hours.

2. **Marinate the lamb:** Combine the sliced lamb with 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, 1 tablespoon Shaoxing rice wine, and 1 teaspoon cornstarch. Set aside to marinate while the dough rests.

3. **Toast and grind the spices:** In a clean, dry pan over medium heat, toast the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and white pepper until fragrant, about 5 minutes. Coarsely grind in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set aside.

4. **Prepare the noodles:** Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Transfer the rested dough to a clean, oiled surface and press into a ½-inch thick rectangle about 10 inches in length. Cut into 10 equal strips. Using your thumbs, press a divot down the center of each strip lengthwise, then hold both ends and gently stretch and slap the noodle against the counter until it is long and wide. Tear the noodle in half at the divot and place on the prepared baking sheet. Repeat with all strips.

5. **Cook the sauce:** Heat 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large wok over medium heat. Add the ginger and scallion whites and cook for 1 minute. Add the garlic and chili pepper and cook for 30 seconds more. Increase heat to high and add the marinated lamb. Stir-fry until just browned.

6. **Finish the sauce:** Immediately add the 2 tablespoons rice wine, prepared spice mixture, chili oil, sugar, black vinegar, soy sauce, salt, red onion, and Chinese celery (if using). If the mixture starts to stick, add ¼ cup of water to deglaze the wok.

7. **Cook the noodles:** While the sauce simmers, drop the noodles and cabbage into a pot of boiling water and cook for 90 seconds to 2 minutes.

8. **Combine and serve:** Transfer the cooked noodles and cabbage directly to the wok along with the garlic chives (if using) and scallion greens. Toss until everything is completely coated in the sauce. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- The noodles hold up well after refrigerating and reheating — leftovers remain chewy.
- Chili oil quantity can be adjusted to taste for more or less heat.
- Chinese celery and garlic chives are optional but add authentic flavor.
