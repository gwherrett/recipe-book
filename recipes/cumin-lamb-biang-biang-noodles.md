# Spicy Cumin Lamb Biang Biang Noodles

**Source:** [The Woks of Life](https://thewoksoflife.com/cumin-lamb-biang-biang-noodles/)

**Prep Time:** 3 hours
**Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**Yield:** 4 servings

Biang biang noodles are a Northern Chinese specialty from Shaanxi Province — wide, thick, chewy, and hand-made, giving them a slightly uneven texture that clings to the signature spicy cumin lamb sauce really well.

---

## Ingredients

### For the Noodles

- 1½ cups bread flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ½ cup water (plus 1 tablespoon)

### For the Lamb & Sauce

- 8 oz. fatty lamb shoulder or chuck, thinly sliced
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, plus 3 tablespoons, divided
- 1 tablespoon rice wine, plus 2 tablespoons, divided
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch
- 1½ tablespoons cumin seeds
- 2 teaspoons coriander seeds
- ¼ teaspoon ground white pepper
- 1 tablespoon grated ginger
- 2 scallions, chopped, white parts and green parts separated
- 7 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 long hot red or green chili pepper, de-seeded and thinly sliced
- 3 tablespoons chili oil (preferably homemade)
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon black vinegar or rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
- ½ cup finely diced Chinese celery or regular celery (optional)
- 3 cabbage leaves, roughly chopped
- ½ cup garlic chives, roughly chopped (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the noodle dough:** In the bowl of a stand mixer, add the bread flour, salt, and water. Combine with your hands or a fork until it starts to form a scraggly dough. Attach the bowl to the mixer fitted with the dough hook and turn it on low speed. Allow the mixer to knead the dough for 20 minutes. By the end, the dough will have formed a ball that doesn't stick to the sides of the bowl, is smooth and elastic, and isn't too dry.

2. **Test and adjust the dough:** You'll know the dough is done when you can rip off a piece and stretch it slowly by a couple inches without it breaking. If the dough breaks, knead for another 3–5 minutes. If it still won't stretch, add a tablespoon of water, knead by hand to incorporate, then transfer back to the mixer for another 5 minutes. If your kitchen is humid and the dough is sticky, sprinkle in more flour and knead for another 3–5 minutes.

3. **Rest the dough:** Cover with plastic wrap or an overturned bowl (not touching the dough) and allow it to rest for 1 to 2 hours.

4. **Marinate the lamb:** Combine the sliced lamb with 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, 1 tablespoon rice wine, and 1 teaspoon cornstarch. Set aside to marinate.

5. **Toast and grind the spices:** In a clean, dry pan over medium heat, add the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and white pepper. Toast until fragrant, about 5 minutes. Coarsely grind in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set aside. Prepare all your vegetables and set those aside as well.

6. **Shape the noodles:** Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and transfer the rested dough to a clean, oiled surface. Press into a ½-inch thick, relatively even rectangle about 10 inches in length. Cut into 10 equal strips. Lay each strip flat and use your palm to flatten it into a long, wide, flat noodle — don't worry about it being perfectly straight or even.

7. **Slap and stretch the noodles:** Pick up each noodle at both ends, lift it, and gently slap it repeatedly on the counter while stretching it out. You can also use your hands to flatten it further so it has a relatively uniform thickness — the noodles should be about the thickness of spaghetti. If they are at all translucent, they're too thin. Lay on the parchment-lined baking sheet, adding another layer of parchment if needed. If you prefer thinner noodles, slice each noodle in half lengthwise with a sharp knife. Cover with plastic wrap so they don't dry out.

8. **Boil the water:** Bring a large pot of water to a boil. The noodles take approximately 90 seconds to cook, so time this so they're done around the same time as the sauce.

9. **Make the sauce:** Heat 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large wok over medium heat. Add the ginger and scallion whites and cook for 1 minute. Add the garlic and chili pepper and cook for another 30 seconds. Crank up the heat to high and add the marinated lamb. Stir-fry until just browned, then immediately add 2 tablespoons rice wine, along with the prepared spice mixture, chili oil, sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, salt, onions, and celery (if using). If things are starting to stick to the bottom of the wok, add ¼ cup or so of water to deglaze it.

10. **Cook the noodles:** While the sauce is going, drop the noodles and cabbage into the boiling water and cook for 90 seconds to 2 minutes.

11. **Combine and serve:** Transfer the cooked noodles and cabbage to the wok along with the garlic chives and scallion greens. Toss until everything is completely coated in the sauce. Serve!
