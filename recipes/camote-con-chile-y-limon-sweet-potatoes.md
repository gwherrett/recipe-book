# Camote con Chile y Limón (Sweet Potatoes with Chile and Lime)

**Category:** Vegetables & Sides  
**Cuisine:** Mexican  
**Tags:** Savory, Spicy, Side Dish, Vegetarian  

## Summary
A simple, savory, and slightly spicy Mexican side dish featuring sweet potatoes sautéed with garlic, chile, and lime. Perfect alongside grilled meats or as a vegetarian taco filling.

## Ingredients (serves 4)
- 2–3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes  
- 2 tbsp olive oil or lard  
- 2 garlic cloves, minced  
- 1–2 fresh serrano or jalapeño chiles, finely chopped (or 1 tsp chile flakes)  
- ½ tsp ground cumin  
- Salt, to taste  
- Juice of 1 lime  
- Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)  

## Method
1. Boil sweet potato cubes in salted water for 8–10 minutes, until just tender. Drain well.  
2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and chiles; sauté until fragrant.  
3. Add sweet potatoes, sprinkle with cumin and salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and crisp on edges (about 8 minutes).  
4. Remove from heat, squeeze lime juice over, and toss gently.  
5. Garnish with cilantro and serve hot.  

## Serving Ideas
- As a side dish with grilled chicken, carne asada, or fish.  
- Stuffed into a taco with queso fresco and salsa verde.  
- Mixed with black beans for a hearty vegetarian plate.

## Notes
- For extra depth, add a pinch of smoked paprika or chipotle powder.
- Substitute butter for olive oil for a richer version.
