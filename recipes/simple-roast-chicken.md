# Simple Roast Chicken

**Source:** [Yotam Ottolenghi's Crowd Pleaser, Menu 1: The Full Roast Dinner](https://www.channel4.com/programmes/ottolenghis-crowd-pleasers)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 55 minutes
**Yield:** 2–4 servings

Spatchcocked and dry-brined, this simple roast chicken delivers crispy skin and juicy meat with minimal effort. The baking soda in the brine draws out moisture for extra browning.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 medium free-range chicken, about 1.7kg
- 1 tbsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp bicarbonate of soda
- 3 onions, peeled and thickly sliced (400g)
- 1 tbsp olive oil

---

## Instructions

1. **Spatchcock the chicken:** Place it breast-side down on a board and pat dry. Using kitchen shears, cut down one side of the backbone, then the other. Remove the backbone and save for stock. Turn the chicken over and spread it open like a book. Press firmly on the breastbone until you hear a crack and the chicken lies flat.

2. **Dry-brine:** Mix the salt with the bicarbonate of soda and season the chicken, first underneath, then over the breast and legs. Leave to dry-brine on a roasting tray in the fridge for up to 12 hours, uncovered, or cook straight away. If refrigerating, allow to come to room temperature before roasting.

3. **Roast:** Arrange the onion slices on the roasting tray all around the chicken. Drizzle the chicken with the oil and roast for 45 minutes at 200°C (fan). Check with a thermometer that the breast closest to the bone reaches 65°C and the thigh joint reaches 80°C. Place under the grill for a couple of minutes for extra colour, if needed.

4. **Rest:** Remove from the oven and rest for 10 minutes before serving.
